#culture 

British work culture

Relationship building
    - <b><u>building rapport and trust</u></b> will colleagues is crucial

    - “how ’s your workload ”


Emphasis on <b><u>achievement</u></b>
    -  British culture <b><u>leans more towards goal orientation</u></b> than process orientation
          + <b><u>they prioritise achieving results</u></b> over strictly following a set procedure


Hierarchy
    -  British work culture tends to be more egalitarian, although there is still respect for authority
    <b><u>Decisions are made with input at various levels</u></b>, and communication can be more informal.

     - brainstorming and ‘putting our heads together’ is commonplace


Flexibility
    - Brits tend to be adaptable and willing to adjust plans as needed
    - “I was thinking we could … Do U think that sounds like a good idea?”


Indirect communication style
    - Brits tend to be more indirect communicators, relying on subtlety and avoiding confrontation

    - East Asian cultures can be more direct and prioritise clear, concise communication


    - “I was just wondering if you could …”
    - “Do u think it would be alright to …”


Work-life balance

Decision-making
    - British decision-making can be more consensual, with a focus on <b><u>discussion and finding common ground</u></b>
