> A successful CEO's role is fundamentally threefold: assembling a high-caliber team, ensuring perpetual capital flow, and defining the overarching organizational vision. These three pillars—people, money, and direction—represent the core levers of scalable enterprise.

### Distillation (2026-05-20) - source: [[../raw/origin-apple-notes/interview work.md]]
**The Engine of Growth:** Each iteration (product version, feature, marketing program) is an attempt to improve the core growth engine. Products must evolve constantly through this optimization process.

**Methodology: Build-Measure-Learn:**
1. Ideas $\rightarrow$ Products (Build)
2. Measure customer response (Measure)
3. Decide whether to persevere or pivot (Learn).

**Application Domains:**
*   **Healthcare:** Decision management systems for hospitals (e.g., visit counts, drug ratios).
*   **Insurance:** AI-enabled risk control through data sharing between hospitals and insurance companies to reduce fraud and improve audit efficiency.

**Core Insight:** Sustainable business success is engineered by rigorously running experiments on the customer base to guide development and prioritization.

### Distillation (2026-05-20) - source: [[../raw/origin-apple-notes/manager.md]]
**Problem Decomposition Hierarchy:**
1. Articulate the inference chain: The process by which a conclusion is reached must be transparently mapped.
2. Locate the problem's stratum: Trace the issue from specific events ($\rightarrow$ pattern $\rightarrow$ system/organization $\rightarrow$ vision).
3. Solution Completeness: When proposing a solution, concurrently present alternative courses of action.

### Distillation (2026-05-20) - source: [[../raw/origin-apple-notes/work insight.md]]
**Core Insights (Level 0 Distillation):**
1. The tendency for intense, competitive thought patterns ('丛林思维') is often perceived as something that others attempt to suppress or contain.
2. The strategic path forward involves achieving financial success ('发财') while maintaining a low profile ('低调').

**Synthesis Note:** This pairing suggests a tension between the necessary internal cognitive intensity required for success and the external requirement for discretion to protect that success.

### Distillation (2026-05-20) - source: [[../raw/origin-apple-notes/career  work.md]]
**Professional Contribution:** Proactively seek external validation regarding one's perceived value by observing peers or soliciting direct feedback. The primary directive must be to dedicate time and energy toward activities that yield tangible business value for the employer and benefit the individual's trajectory. 

**Focus and Mindset:** When facing professional stagnation or difficulty, the emotional response (gratitude or frustration) is valid, but the amplified outcome is determined solely by where one directs their energy. Therefore, intentional focus dictates the experienced reality.

### Distillation (2026-05-20) - source: [[../raw/origin-apple-notes/insight education.md]]
**Environmental Agency:** A successful environment is not passively received; it must be actively constructed through the efforts of all involved parties (parents, teachers, self). This applies equally to academic success and professional ventures.

**Market Acuity:** High-tech ventures, regardless of their sophistication, must remain tethered to tangible economic realities, such as consumer purchasing power and income levels. The key to success, as seen in the case of Hudson, is the ability to foresee market trends and locate service gaps.

**Fairness and Rationality:** A strong sense of fairness is a valuable trait, but it must be tempered with absolute rationality when calculating the impact of one's actions. This requires assuming the counterpart also operates under a framework of mutual consideration.

### Distillation (2026-05-20) - source: [[../raw/origin-apple-notes/idea business.md]]
**Execution Velocity vs. Perfection:** The Muskian approach suggests that achieving initial deployment and iterating afterward is superior to achieving theoretical perfection upfront. This prioritizes market feedback and momentum.

**Leadership and Followership:** Leadership is situational; one must possess the capacity to lead when the situation demands it, but also the wisdom to follow when a superior path is identified. This requires situational awareness and intellectual humility.

**Action Over Analysis:** To escape the trap of overthinking, the antidote is decisive action. True success is defined by clear intent followed by execution, not by prolonged contemplation.

### Distillation (2026-05-20) - source: [[../raw/origin-apple-notes/business  important  insight  self.md]]
**组织政治敏感度 (Organizational Political Savvy)**: This is a trainable skill requiring the ability to project confidence and professional diplomacy while maintaining genuine sincerity to build trust across organizational strata.
**Trust & Relationships**: Trust is earned through consistent demonstration of sincerity and deep insight into human nature. High-quality friendships offer profound warmth and effortless understanding.
**Personal Positioning**: One must maintain absolute clarity regarding personal goals—what one intends to achieve, what one is capable of, and the path forward. The core skill is '安生立命' (establishing one's foundation calmly), meaning the ability to create value for others regardless of the setting.

**Key Competencies for Success:**
1. **Authenticity**: Displaying sincerity and genuineness to inspire confidence.
2. **Connectivity**: Effectively building relationships across all levels of the organization and external bodies.
3. **Clarity of Purpose**: Knowing one's goals, capabilities, and ultimate trajectory.

**Cautionary Note**: Do not allow yourself to be the passive recipient of choice; maintain agency over your own trajectory.

### Distillation (2026-05-20) - source: [[../raw/diary/business/2020-04-13-the-practical-part-in-business.md]]
**Selling the Skill as a Service (Scaling Model):**
- Learn a skill $\rightarrow$ Sell the skill as a service $\rightarrow$ Build a business around it $\rightarrow$ Remove yourself from the operational business $\rightarrow$ Scale.

**Employee Value Proposition (EVP):**
- Must address benefits, compensation, careers, well-being, and purpose. Crucially, plan for the worst while hoping for the best.

**Startup CEO Role:**
- Hire generalists initially to build, then hire specialists for scaling.

**Vision vs. Comparison:**
- When your vision becomes clear, comparison to others becomes irrelevant. Concentrate energy on your own path.

**Creativity and Market Value:**
- Creativity applied to problem-solving is what free markets pay for. If a task doesn't require creativity, delegate, automate, or discard it.

**Modern Market Dynamics (Sharing Economy):**
- Competition, openness, and sharing are keys to collective advancement. Sharing builds reputation (resume) and allows for larger, higher-quality projects through specialization.
- **Caution on Sharing:** Do not share your 'secret sauce' (e.g., Google search algorithms).

**Business Mindset:**
- Business is a game of making money; avoid zero-sum games. Treat partners as allies attempting to win together.
- Business interests should be maximized unless fundamental human safety/freedom is threatened.

**Practical Execution Flow (Planning Power):**
- **Idea $\rightarrow$ Concept $\rightarrow$ Feasibility Plan $\rightarrow$ Design $\rightarrow$ Development $\rightarrow$ Launch.**
- Start by doing everything manually to establish the baseline, then automate for scale.

**Intuition and Decision Making:**
- Intuition requires establishing a calm, detached observing self. Separate genuine intuition from wishful thinking or projection by rigorously questioning the source of the feeling.

**Goal Alignment:**
- Connect every goal to a fundamental purpose (the 'why').

**Traceability:** (Source: [[diary/business/2020-04-13-the-practical-part-in-business.md]])

### Distillation (2026-05-20) - source: [[../raw/diary/diary/2023-09-04-the-log-of-your-life.md]]
**Sales & Pitching:**
- Seek feedback on your pitch.
- Prioritize listening over speaking.
- Center the discussion on how you can help the customer.
- Let ego aside.
- **Closing:** Ask permission to move forward (e.g., "Are you ready to take the next step?").
- **Long Term:** Sales is rarely one-and-done; maintain contact.

**Asset Building (Chinese Context):**
- Build assets first; use income from assets to purchase luxuries.
- Business focus should be on building assets, not just generating income.
- Use extra income to acquire income-producing assets.
- **Acquiring Desired Assets:** Increase assets through stocks and real estate.
- If you love your investment, understand it and the rules; risk decreases.
- *Personal Note:* I prefer small company stocks, especially startups, as an entrepreneur.
- **Corporate Form:** Provides benefits and protection (legal entity).

**Small Business Profitability:**
- Aim for high-margin businesses.
- Low marginal cost and infinitely replicable operational models/products maximize profit.
- High-margin businesses maintain constant operating costs regardless of volume.

**Doing Business in the US:**
- Remember two things: Legal employment + Buy insurance (buy as much as possible).
- This removes the fear of unexpected events; employees are covered by insurance claims.

**Other Notes:**
- Retail brokerage company: webull.

**Cognitive Observations (Chinese Contextualization):**
- **Chinese Mindset:** Many believe they can be the 'house owner' (庄家) for others, leading to risk accumulation.
- **Chinese Traits:** High tolerance for lies; focus on 'pros and cons' rather than right/wrong; transactional view ('Is this advantageous to me?').
- **Driving Safety Protocol (Analogy):** Before merging/passing, check all mirrors (side, rear, rearview); signal; execute the maneuver.

**The Three Pillars of CEO Function:**
1. **Team Building:** Identifying and cultivating a superior group of individuals.
2. **Capital Management:** Maintaining and aggressively growing financial resources.
3. **Direction Setting:** Establishing and communicating the company's ultimate vision and objectives.
