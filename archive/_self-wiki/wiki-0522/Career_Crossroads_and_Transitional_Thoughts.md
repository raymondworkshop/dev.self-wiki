### Distillation (2026-05-20) - source: [[../raw/diary/self/2020-03-20-update-my-career.md]]
**Core Competencies & Vision:**
- Career success is defined by the application of one's skills (language, programming, philosophy, communication, aesthetics).
- Leadership involves executing a vision with a team.
- Specialization is required; one must excel in what they are good at.

**Contextualizing Career Paths (The Pragmatic View):**
- For those without significant initial advantages, self-reliance and clear self-positioning are paramount.
- The relationship between individual and organization dictates the driver: Employee = Wage; Mid-to-High Level = Vision/Goal; Partner = Destiny/Shared Energy & Capital.
- Financial viability is a necessary consideration, not always secondary to learning.

**Self-Knowledge & Professional Conduct:**
- Deep self-awareness regarding strengths (e.g., empathy, communication) and weaknesses is crucial.
- Success hinges on the ability to complete tasks excellently from start to finish and collaborate effectively with the team.
- **Lessons Learned (HSUHK Experience):** Gained courage and improved communication skills through challenging interactions.

**Qualities of a Good Fit (The 'Good Work' Filter):**
- **People:** Shared values, genuine connection, and mutual alignment on ideals are vital.
- **Opportunity:** The work must offer growth, challenge, and the chance to learn unknown skills.
- **Autonomy:** One must be honest about who they are working for and feel the compensation/outcome is fair.

**Refinement/Cautionary Notes:**
- **Soft Skills:** When offering advice, lead with validation of feeling before seeking solutions; avoid being overly blunt.
- **Interpersonal Boundaries:** Recognize and maintain boundaries with those who are difficult, insecure, or lacking in depth.

**Actionable Mindset Shifts:**
- **Time Management:** Always calculate the time cost of any endeavor.
- **Career View:** One must strive to be an agent of their own work, not merely an employee.
- **Information Gathering:** Seek high-quality, vetted information (e.g., academic databases) rather than relying on anecdotal advice.
