> The self is not discovered; it is actively created through choices made in daily life. A truly vital individual shapes themselves against conventional norms, much like an artist creates a work of art. Therefore, the pursuit of self is the pursuit of constant self-creation.



### Distillation (2026-05-20) - source: [[../raw/origin-apple-notes/thinking  self  philogra.md]]
The self is not a pre-given entity to be found, but rather something that must be created through the accumulation of daily choices and practices. 

*   **The Blank Slate:** An individual begins without fixed rules or norms; the self is fluid and contingent upon action. 
*   **The Artist Analogy:** The ideal self is the one who strives to invent themselves, not merely to uncover a hidden 'truth.' 
*   **Action vs. Inaction:** Conformity stems from a lack of creative drive; genuine individuality arises from altering established norms through self-shaping.
*   **The Mandate:** If the self is not given, the only viable outcome is that we must consciously create ourselves, much like an artist brings a work into existence.
