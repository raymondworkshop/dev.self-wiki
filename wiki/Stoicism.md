Stoicism is a practical philosophy focused on self-mastery, resilience, and living in accordance with nature and reason. It emphasizes the "dichotomy of control," distinguishing between what we can and cannot influence to maintain tranquility.

### Core Principles

#### The Dichotomy of Control
Stoicism's most fundamental teaching is distinguishing between things we can control and things we cannot.
- **Can control**: Our opinions, goals, values, and our own actions.
- **Cannot control**: External events, other people's opinions, the past, and outcomes of our actions.
*Strategy*: Focus exclusively on what is within our control to avoid frustration and maintain [[Self]]-governance.

#### Acceptance of Responsibility and Perseverance
- **Ownership of Action**: A core principle of existence is the acceptance of responsibility for one's own path. 
- **Perseverance**: Success requires the courage to stand and fight for one's values, starting over each morning as the sun rises.
- **Strive for Excellence**: Happiness comes from the full use of one's power to achieve excellence. Living with excellence is a defense against mediocrity.

#### Living in the Moment
The past is unchangeable, and the future is uncertain. Stoics emphasize "Amor Fati" (loving one's fate) and focusing on the present moment.
- "The past is cannot be changed; the future is uncertain."
- Focus on doing what is right *now*.

#### Social Duty and Human Connection
Despite its focus on internal tranquility, Stoicism is deeply social. We have a duty to serve humanity and maintain relationships, even with difficult people.
- "Loving humanity is our obligation."
- We are social animals born to work together in a mutually beneficial way.

### Practical Applications

#### Dealing with Difficult People and Insults
- **Social Fate**: Realize that some people are naturally difficult; it is their nature.
- **Humor and Indifference**: Respond to insults with humor (self-deprecation) or simply ignore them.
- **Consider the Source**: If an insult is true, there is no reason to be upset; if it is false, the insulter is simply mistaken.

#### Overcoming Destructive Emotions
- **Anger**: View anger as a temporary madness. Punishment should be for correction, not an expression of anger.
- **Grief**: Use "Negative Visualization" to prepare for loss, and rely on reason to mitigate excessive sorrow.
- **Fame**: Do not seek external validation. True worth comes from knowing who you are, not from others' opinions.

### Key Figures and Texts
- **Marcus Aurelius**: *Meditations* - A series of personal reflections on duty and nature.
- **Seneca**: Letters and essays on tranquility and the shortness of life.
- **Epictetus**: *Enchiridion* and *Discourses* - Focused on the practical application of Stoic logic.
