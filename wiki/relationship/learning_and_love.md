This page synthesizes views on the pursuit of shared meaning, the dynamics of intimacy, and the prerequisites for genuine connection, applying lessons from both personal relationships and intellectual pursuits. It stresses that true liking requires accepting the totality of the other.

## Evolution
Initial notes touched upon the mechanics of learning and the need to overcome stalemates. This expanded into a holistic view of love, where acceptance of imperfection and the ability to build shared meaning are central.

## Content
### Love and Partnership
*   **Foundation:** Begin with friendship, developing from shared interests.
*   **Intimacy:** Requires the effort to accept the partner's pain and inadequacies.
*   **Security:** The ability to be oneself in the presence of the other.
*   **Totality:** If you like a person or something, you must like their entirety, as they represent the sum total of the relationship.
*   **Essentials:** Sincerity, safety, and acceptance.

### Learning and Life
*   **Shared Meaning:** The pursuit of creating a shared meaning in life.
*   **Problem Solving:** Overcoming deadlocks and resolving solvable issues.
*   **Influence:** Accepting the influence of the partner/other.
*   **Attitude:** Those with faith must be good people: honest and upright.
*   **Daily Life:** "Everyday is the first day of the rest of your life"; every day is a compound day.
*   **Perspective:** Life has ups and downs. Humility in success, confidence in difficulty. The ability to climb mountains is a skill; helping others climb is leadership.

### Self-Reflection Practices
*   **Emotional Processing:** When encountering difficult situations, reflect in the evening, write down your feelings, and clarify the root cause of the emotion to gain deeper self-knowledge.
*   **External Focus:** Consider how to bring joy to others, including developing a sense of humor and the ability to listen attentively to others' voices.
*   **Business Acumen:** Identify products with 80% profitability.
*   **Decision Making:** Major life decisions must be absolutely rational, setting aside emotional or preferential biases. Money is not the primary factor; the pursuit of professionalism and skill is what earns respect. Consider pivoting only when a positive opportunity arises.
