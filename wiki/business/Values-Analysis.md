Your values system is a **highly rationalized self-evolution system** built on "Dynamic Empiricism" and "Conscious Architecture." It represents a "Stoic Architect" persona that transforms environmental randomness and emotional volatility into structured growth feedback.

### 1. Core Values: Autonomy and Inner Freedom (The Seat of Awareness)
The foundational value is the pursuit of **spiritual independence**. This involves a Stoic separation between the "Witness (Awareness)" and the "Ego," achieving equanimity through conscious observation rather than reactive emotion.
*   **Evidence**: Repeated emphasis on "Witness Consciousness" and the "Internal Iceberg" in [[Self-Knowledge-and-Personal-Growth]].
*   **Insight**: Freedom is not doing whatever you want, but the ability to choose your response to any given stimulus (Victor Frankl).

### 2. Operational Logic: Bayesian Life View (Dynamic Empiricism)
You view life as a series of tests and revisions rather than a static plan.
*   **Bottom-up Framework**: You prioritize gathering "experience fragments" first, then constructing frameworks that are always ready to be falsified or updated with new data.
*   **Market Logic**: You apply economic principles (market value, asset allocation, competition) to social and interpersonal dynamics, trusting only value that has been tested by reality.

### 3. Management Values: Professionalism and Contribution (Executive Effectiveness)
In the professional sphere, your standards are uncompromising: **results over effort, contribution over activity.**
*   **Evidence**: Heavy influence from Peter Drucker, emphasizing "What can I contribute?" and "First things first" in [[Work]].
*   **Insight**: You advocate for "Instruction Manuals" for all processes to achieve de-personalized, high-efficiency management.

### 4. Cognitive Values: Antifragility and Randomness
You embrace complexity and unpredictability, seeking to gain from disorder.
*   **Evidence**: [[Antifragile]] principles are applied across disciplines, from [[Technology]] to social observation in [[China]].
*   **Insight**: Trial and error is the primary method of learning; failure is a necessary feedback mechanism, not a source of shame.

### 5. Emotional Values: Labeling and Processing (RULER Model)
Emotions are treated as "messengers" to be decoded rather than suppressed.
*   **Evidence**: Implementation of the RULER model for naming and understanding internal states.
*   **Insight**: The goal is a "Heart and Mind Balance" where the "Rational Brain" executes actions informed by the "Emotional Spark."

### Core Paradox: Integrity vs. Softness
Your system balances "Inner Integrity" (strict core principles) with "Outer Softness" (polite, strategic social compromise). This "Stoic Architect" approach protects your creative core while navigating the "noisy" external world.
