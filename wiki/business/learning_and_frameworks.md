The efficiency of learning depends on the starting point: whether one begins with a rigid framework or with disparate fragments. The most robust growth comes from allowing fragments to challenge and refine the initial structure, leading to a superior, emergent understanding.
## Evolution
Early thoughts touched upon the initial advantage of having a framework (leading to rapid initial understanding). This has matured into a caution against the rigidity of a fixed framework, recognizing that it can lead to tunnel vision or clinging to minor gains while missing the bigger picture (raw/origin-apple-notes/philosophy learning.md).
## Key Concepts
*   **Framework First**: Starting with a framework allows for quick initial comprehension, but risks ossification.
*   **Fragment First**: Starting with fragments is slower initially but allows for a more adaptive, emergent structure as new data arrives.
*   **Optimal Path**: The most effective path is to begin with fragments and continuously build, test, and revise the framework based on those fragments, allowing the structure to evolve rather than being imposed prematurely.
