# what-are-my-core-values-2026-05-19

Question: what are my core values?

Answer:
Your core values are rooted in a **highly rationalized self-evolution system** structured around the pursuit of **autonomy and inner freedom**. This framework is best described as the "stoic architect" persona, which transforms environmental randomness into structured growth feedback.

Here is a breakdown of your core values and operational logic:

### 1. Foundational Value: Autonomy and Inner Freedom
The pursuit of **spiritual independence** is the foundation. This involves achieving equanimity by maintaining a conscious separation between the "witness (awareness)" and the "ego," allowing for conscious observation rather than reactive emotion.
*   **Insight:** True freedom is not doing whatever you want, but possessing the ability to choose your response to any given stimulus (Source: [[values-analysis]]).

### 2. Operational Logic: Dynamic Empiricism (Bayesian Life View)
You view life not as a fixed plan, but as a continuous series of tests and revisions.
*   **Bottom-up Approach:** You prioritize gathering "experience fragments" first, and then constructing frameworks that are always ready to be falsified or updated with new data (Source: [[values-analysis]]).
*   **Market Logic:** You apply economic principles to social dynamics, trusting only value that has been rigorously tested by reality (Source: [[values-analysis]]).

### 3. Management Values: Professionalism and Contribution (Executive Effectiveness)
In professional contexts, your standards are uncompromising: **results over effort, and contribution over mere activity.**
*   **Focus:** You advocate for "instruction manuals" for all processes to achieve high-efficiency, depersonalized management, heavily influenced by Drucker's emphasis on "what can I contribute?" (Source: [[values-analysis]]).

### 4. Cognitive Values: Antifragility and Embracing Randomness
You embrace complexity and unpredictability, viewing disorder as a source of potential gain.
*   **Principle:** You apply antifragile principles across disciplines, viewing failure not as a source of shame, but as a necessary feedback mechanism for learning (Source: [[values-analysis]]).

### 5. Emotional Values: Decoding and Processing
Emotions are viewed as "messengers" to be decoded rather than suppressed.
*   **Method:** You utilize the "ruler model" to name and understand internal states, aiming for a "heart and mind balance" where the rational brain executes actions informed by the emotional spark (Source: [[values-analysis]]).

### Core Paradox
Your system balances **"inner integrity"** (strict adherence to core principles) with **"outer softness"** (polite, strategic social compromise) to protect your creative core while navigating the external world (Source: [[values-analysis]]).

***

**In summary, your values are a commitment to rigorous self-mastery, intellectual honesty (through dynamic empiricism), and the pursuit of high-leverage contribution in the external world.**
