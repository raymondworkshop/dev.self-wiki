> This note contrasts Rationalism (deductive logic from first principles) with Empiricism (inductive logic from sensory observation), mapping these philosophical positions onto modern scientific methodology and legal systems. It emphasizes that scientific progress requires a willingness to fail and revise frameworks based on new empirical data, echoing the Bayesian approach.



### Distillation (2026-05-20) - source: [[../raw/diary/learning/2022-01-24-notes-on-philosophy-and-thinking.md]]
**可证伪性 (Falsifiability):** Modern science advances through a cycle of hypothesis testing and failure, which contrasts with the human desire for certainty. The journey to understanding the world is inherently a process of refinement, not immediate arrival at truth.

**先验理性 vs 经验理性 (A Priori vs. Empirical Reason):**
- **先验 (Rationalism):** Deductive logic derived from definitions and first principles; knowledge exists in a perfect, independent realm.
- **经验 (Empiricism):** Inductive logic derived from observed evidence; knowledge is built from sensory experience.

**实证主义 (Positivism) & 科学方法 (Scientific Method):**
- The scientific process moves from observation $\rightarrow$ pattern recognition $\rightarrow$ short-cut framework $\rightarrow$ testing/falsification. This mirrors the Bayesian approach: initial fragments build a framework, which is then constantly corrected or broken by new fragments.
- **证伪主义 (Falsificationism):** Science's role is to seek observations that contradict the current theory, thereby testing its limits.

**Legal Analogy (判例法):**
- Common law relies on empirical observation and inductive reasoning, allowing for greater adaptability to social dynamics and providing a 'feedback' mechanism, contrasting with codified law's pursuit of stability.

**Cognitive vs. Emotional Intelligence:**
- Analytical thinking tends toward reason; Emotional Intelligence involves discerning the emotions and motives of others. 

**Philosophical Application:**
- Extreme views (both political/moral) often ignore the causal chain and jump directly from phenomenon to moral answer, neglecting the necessary nuance in the problem itself.
