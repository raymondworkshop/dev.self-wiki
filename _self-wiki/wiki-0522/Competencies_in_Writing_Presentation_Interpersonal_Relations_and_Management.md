### Distillation (2026-05-20) - source: [[../raw/diary/softskills/2020-03-24-build-good-relationships.md]]
**Mentors:** Seek out individuals whose qualities you admire and integrate those strengths into your own approach. Approach mentorship proactively by setting the agenda and asking insightful questions to maximize the value of the interaction.

**Managing Your Manager:** Proactivity is key. Always enter one-on-ones prepared with a list of topics. Seek clarity on role expectations by asking about successful examples and deliverables. Solicit performance feedback graciously, framing it as a joint postmortem to show commitment to improvement.

**Peers:** Treat peers as powerful advocates. Success requires letting go of control, understanding each peer's motivations, and approaching collaboration as a collective effort ("us"). Build trust through sincere recognition and by proactively offering support before expecting it in return.

**Core Principles:** All interactions—with mentors, managers, or peers—must be approached with preparation, active listening, and a willingness to contribute solutions alongside problems. The goal is to build authentic, mutually beneficial relationships, prioritizing quality over quantity in associations.

### Distillation (2026-05-20) - source: [[../raw/diary/learning/2025-03-18-managing-up.md]]
**Managing Your Boss (The Partnership Model)**
- View the relationship as a mutual partnership requiring shared success and influence.
- **Performance Check:** Are expectations being met regarding targets, information flow, and loyalty?
- **Information Flow:** Develop a clear understanding of what your boss needs to know.
- **Alignment & Trust:** Negotiate the shared definition of "doing the right thing." Ensure goals and plans are known and agreed upon by your boss.
- **Proactive Expectation Setting:** Begin assignments by negotiating expectations and periodically updating them. Warn of potential risks and model various handling scenarios.
- **Perspective Taking:** Seek to understand the world through your boss's viewpoint and identify their core strengths.
- **Support & Evaluation:** Test the boss's willingness to support versus evaluate. Learn their management philosophy (e.g., planning, decisiveness).

**Managing Up (The Wider View)**
- **Interacting with Her Boss:** Treat the boss's superior as a key stakeholder. Maintain professional presence (greeting, offering help).
- **Visibility & Support:** Offer congratulations or relevant resources to the boss's superior when appropriate.
- **Seeking Counsel:** Use the boss's superior for advice, framing it to reflect positively on your direct boss.

**Core Mandate:** The ultimate goal is to enhance your boss's reputation with their superior through your professional conduct and proactive support.

### Distillation (2026-05-20) - source: [[../raw/diary/learning/2017-11-17-improve-your-persuasive-skills.md]]
**Interviews as Foundational Tools:** Interviews serve as primary mechanisms for listening, learning, problem-solving, and establishing rapport.

**Questioning Taxonomy:**
- **Close-ended:** Pinpointing specific facts or gauging unwillingness to articulate.
- **Open-ended:** Used after establishing context, often employing 'what/why' to elicit deeper thought.
- **Strategic Questions:**
    - **Summary Questions:** Validate understanding and demonstrate active listening (e.g., "Let me check if I understand your points...").
    - **Leading Questions:** Can pivot the conversation but carry inherent bias.
    - **Clearinghouse Questions:** Essential for identifying key stakeholders (e.g., "Who else should I talk to?").

**Contextual Styles & Roles:**
- **Manager:** Must engage with the individual's personality.
- **Caretaker:** Focuses on validation and ensuring the speaker feels heard.
- **Analyst:** Values accuracy and is slow to speak but quick to process.
- **Director:** Decisive, ambitious, and task-oriented.
- **Entertainer:** Idea-rich and enjoys collaborative contribution.

**Deep Listening Skills (The Art of Being Heard):**
- Many individuals seek validation and approval, not just 'an opinion.' The best way to persuade is often through deep listening.
- **Principle of Empathy:** To truly understand, one must attempt to inhabit the other person's frame of reference.
- **Active Listening Techniques:**
    - **Mirroring:** Repeating the last few words or the gist of the speaker's point.
    - **Encouragement:** Using verbal cues ("uh-huh," nodding) to allow full expression.
    - **Summarizing:** Demonstrating perspective-taking and seeking clarification.

**Advanced Communication Dynamics:**
- **Negative Assumption Questioning:** Starting with "What problems does it have?" is often the most truthful path, as it signals the presence of an issue and demonstrates deep knowledge.
- **Effective Messaging:** To grab attention, the message must be simple, vivid, and memorable (using analogy or concrete examples).
