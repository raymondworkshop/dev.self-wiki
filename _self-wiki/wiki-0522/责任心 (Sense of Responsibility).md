> This concept appears to be a foundational principle regarding commitment and duty, particularly in the context of value exchange and professional roles. It requires a clear understanding of one's obligations to maintain integrity in interactions.



### Distillation (2026-05-20) - source: [[../raw/origin-apple-notes/value.md]]
The core of this principle lies in the alignment between stated commitments and executed actions. It transcends mere task completion; it involves a cognitive acceptance of the relational weight of one's word and output. 

**Distillation Points:**
1. **Value Exchange Integrity:** Recognizing that all interactions involve a transaction of value, and one's responsibility is to ensure the exchange is honest and complete. (Source: [[origin-apple-notes/value.md]])
2. **Self-Perception Alignment:** The execution of duties reinforces or degrades one's self-concept. A failure in duty is a failure in self-definition. (Source: [[origin-apple-notes/value.md]])
3. **Scope of Duty:** Responsibility must be clearly bounded. Unbounded commitment leads to diffusion and burnout. (AI Synthesis: Requires mapping this against 'Boundary Setting' principles.)
