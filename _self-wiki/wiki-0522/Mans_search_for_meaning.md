> The search for meaning is inherently individual, requiring each person to answer the question of their own life. Logotherapy posits that this meaning can be found through creating work, experiencing profound encounters (like love), or bravely accepting unavoidable suffering.



### Distillation (2026-05-26) - source: [[../raw/diary/2018-04-25-mans-search-for-meaning.md]]
The primary motivation in life is the search for unique, individual meaning. This meaning is not found externally but is the answer to the question posed by life itself. Logotherapy identifies three avenues for discovering this meaning:

1. **Creation/Deed**: Finding purpose through tangible accomplishment or work.
2. **Experience/Encounter**: Experiencing objective values (beauty, truth) or deep connection with another unique being, which allows for mutual actualization of potential.
3. **Attitude toward Suffering**: Finding meaning by bravely accepting and confronting unavoidable hardship.

Life's potentialities are transitory until they are actualized, at which point they become preserved realities. Logotherapy views this process not as pessimistic, but as an active engagement with existence.
