> Leadership is defined as the practice of mobilizing people to meet tough challenges, which requires building new capacity within the group. True leadership involves diagnosing the challenge to distinguish technical problems from adaptive ones, where the people themselves are part of the problem.

### Distillation (2026-05-20) - source: [[../raw/origin-apple-notes/leadership.md]]
**Core Definition of Leadership:** The practice of mobilizing people to meet tough challenges, which inherently involves building something new.

**Adaptive Challenges vs. Technical Problems:**
*   **Technical Problems:** Can be solved by applying existing knowledge or resources.
*   **Adaptive Challenges:** Require fundamental change in mindset or behavior. The people involved are part of the problem, and the solution must come from them.

**The Leader's Role (Diagnostic Task):**
1.  **Framing:** Accurately framing the right questions and identifying key realities.
2.  **Diagnosis:** Distinguishing between technical components (solvable with current capacity) and adaptive components (requiring behavioral shift).
3.  **Facilitation:** Challenging individuals to take responsibility for addressing the adaptive components.

**Key Insight:** Leadership is the practice of facilitating adaptive work—creating with people the new capacity needed to thrive in a changing world.

### Distillation (2026-05-20) - source: [[../raw/origin-apple-notes/我能够沿途指出拿破仑的天才如何产生效力，尤其是下列品质的重要性：.md]]
**Core Competencies for Effective Leadership (Napoleon Model):**
1. **Planning & Execution:** Meticulous planning, deep study of terrain, and seizing opportune moments.
2. **Command Presence:** Maintaining composure, discipline, and focus, especially when facing potential defeat.
3. **Team Cohesion:** Understanding the psychology of the average soldier to build esprit de corps.
4. **Strategic Application:** Ability to learn from modern tactics, ask precise questions, and, when necessary, exercise ruthless pragmatism.
5. **Detail & Temperament:** Near-obsessive attention to detail combined with rigorous emotional self-management.
6. **Critical Mass:** The ultimate application of temporary troop superiority at the decisive point.

*Note: The inclusion of 'good luck' is noted as an external variable, but the internal qualities are the primary drivers of scalable leadership.*

### Distillation (2026-05-20) - source: [[../raw/diary/learning/2020-03-16-on-leadership-and-teamwork.md]]
**Core Dynamics of Influence (Warmth + Strength):**
- **Warmth (Belonging):** Begin by validating feelings and acknowledging concerns (e.g., "I know everybody's feeling a lot of uncertainty right now"). This builds the necessary foundation for followership.
- **Strength (Competence):** This is demonstrated by maintaining a grounded presence during stress and possessing a clear vision. It is about connecting with your own command and confidence.

**The Role of the Leader (Beyond Heroics):**
- **Coaching over Solving:** The most advanced leadership is teaching team members how to solve problems themselves. This involves asking insightful, challenging questions that require thought, rather than simply serving up a solution.
- **Situational Awareness:** A leader must constantly assess the situation, the team's readiness, and the optimal tone required for the moment.

**Decision Making & Team Function:**
- **Process vs. Content:** A team functions best when both the 'content' (Is the goal clear? Do we have the skills?) and the 'process' (Are roles clear? Is communication open?) are addressed.
- **Decision Courage:** Making a decision, even if flawed, is better than remaining in a state of liminal opportunity. This requires courage and the acceptance that all decisions are micro-level adaptations to a specific context.

**Cognitive Pitfalls to Avoid:**
- **Confirmation Bias:** Seeking only information that supports existing beliefs.
- **Decision Paralysis:** Allowing the pursuit of perfect information to prevent necessary action.
