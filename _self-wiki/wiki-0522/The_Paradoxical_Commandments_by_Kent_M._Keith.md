> This document captures Kent M. Keith's Paradoxical Commandments, which serve as enduring philosophical anchors against the transient nature of success, failure, and human interaction. They compel the practitioner to maintain virtuous action regardless of immediate outcome or external validation.



### Distillation (2026-05-20) - source: [[../raw/diary/self/2017-10-05-commandments.md]]
'What you spend years building may be destroyed overnight. Build anyway.'
'The good you do today will be forgotten tomorrow. Do good anyway.'
'If you are successful, you will win false friends and true enemies. Succeed anyway.'
'If you do good, people will accuse you of selfish ulterior motives. Do good anyway.'

(Source: [[diary/self/2017-10-05-commandments.md]])
