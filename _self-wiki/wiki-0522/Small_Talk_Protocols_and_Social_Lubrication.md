### Distillation (2026-05-20) - source: [[../raw/origin-apple-notes/english.md]]
**Greetings & Check-ins:** Acceptable variations include "How’s it going?", "How are you?", or "You alright?" followed by brief, positive affirmations like "I’m good" or "Pretty good." The reciprocal question is typically "You?" or "Yourself?".
**Weather Commentary:** Standardized phrases range from "Lovely weather, isn’t it?" to acknowledging poor conditions like "Miserable." Responses must match the observed reality (e.g., "It’s pouring" vs. "There’s a bit of rain.").
**Status Update:** The default response to "What’s new?" is often "Not much," followed by a reciprocal inquiry to maintain conversational flow. The underlying principle noted is the avoidance of ostentation (​) during these exchanges.

**Principle: Navigating Social Encounters**
1. **Specificity over Generality**: Address the immediate topic at hand with clarity and sincerity. (事有必較)
2. **Boundary Maintenance**: Do not use initial pleasantries as an opportunity to pry into personal affairs or seek deep intimacy prematurely. (勿問人私事，勿以套近乎)

*This principle balances the need for social cohesion with the necessity of individual autonomy.*

### Distillation (2026-05-20) - source: [[../raw/origin-apple-notes/socialskills.md]]
**Foundational Premise: Self-Care as Professional Duty**
The capacity to be a valuable team member is directly proportional to the discipline applied to one's physical, emotional, and mental well-being. One must establish competence in their own domain before effectively engaging with others.

**Protocol for Interpersonal Engagement**
1. **Intentional Outreach**: Proactively schedule interactions (virtual or in-person) with colleagues. These interactions should extend beyond transactional tasks.
2. **Relational Depth**: Initiate conversations regarding personal domains (pets, recent moves, family) to move beyond surface-level exchanges.
3. **Active Listening**: The primary skill during these exchanges must be deep, non-judgmental reception of the other person's narrative.
4. **Recognition and Value**: Authentic acknowledgment of the other person's unique attributes signals that they are being seen as a valued individual, which solidifies social bonds.
5. **Feedback Integrity**: When offering critique, it must be framed as a benevolent contribution aimed at mutual betterment, not mere criticism.
