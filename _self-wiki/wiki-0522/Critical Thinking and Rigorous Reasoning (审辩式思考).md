> Critical thinking, in its rigorous form, is the pursuit of the most rational and objective judgment by grounding thought in facts and logic. This disciplined approach is crucial for building sound reasoning structures that achieve mutual understanding and acceptance.

### Distillation (2026-05-20) - source: [[../raw/origin-apple-notes/research.md]]
Critical thinking (审辩式思考) is primarily aimed at achieving the most rational and objective judgment possible. It serves to establish a rigorous and solid chain of reasoning, making it easier for others to understand and accept.

This process is founded upon: Facts and Logic $\rightarrow$ Rational and Objective Judgment.

In the context of scientific research, this involves:
*   Attention to the history of science within the relevant field when selecting a topic.
*   Dialectical analysis of one's own experimental results.
*   Maintaining excellent experimental habits.

当信息或观点出现偏差时，不应直接否定，而应将其视为一个需要被探究的现象。追问“为什么会是这样”（Why is this so?）是保持开放心智、进行深度认知映射的有效切入点。这使得认知冲突转化为建设性的、可被分解的探究对象。

**Research & Problem-Solving Methodology:**
- **Research Goal:** To train the ability to retrieve information, reason based on sufficient data, and construct an independent theoretical framework to reach a conclusion.
- **Problem-Solving System:** Establish a method system based on current knowledge; apply it to problems, identify insufficiencies, and allow the system to evolve. When evolution stalls, engage in extensive reading to borrow novel methods.
- **Addressing Complex/Unknown Problems:** The ultimate goal must be defined: what is the deeper value of the task to the organization? One must possess the vision to transcend the current framework and address deeper, more complex issues.
- **Idea Generation (构想力):** The ability to accurately constrain a problem into a highly useful sub-problem, defining the precise focus and the necessary level of abstraction (how much to omit) is key. The breakthrough often comes from adding novel elements to an initial idea.
- **Critical Thinking:** This is a constructive, dialectical judgment. Beyond logical/statistical correctness, it requires asking questions no one has asked and digging into fundamental causes.
- **Correlation vs. Causation:** Most current research establishes correlation; using this correlation to search for underlying mechanisms is the basis for inferring causation. Stating that all correlation studies are unscientific is overly restrictive.

**Idea Lifecycle & Execution:**
- **Idea Stages:** Mini-proposal (3 suggestions, 1 sentence) $\rightarrow$ Proposal (1 page: idea + pursuit plan) $\rightarrow$ Partial Draft (3 pages: Intro, background, baseline results, lit review) $\rightarrow$ Final Paper (4 pages).
- **Independent Researcher:** Independence does not mean isolation; it means possessing the ownership to collect knowledge, form ideas, and engage in dialogue with those who can assist.

**Academic/Research Context:**
- **Improving Taste (品味):** To judge important work, one could study the history of science (e.g., reviewing Turing Award recipients) or analyze highly cited papers.
- **Idea Generation (Practical):** 1) Select an area. 2) Find a paper; check its citations/cited works. 3) Keep reading until a question arises: "What if I tried X?" $\rightarrow$ Try X.

### Distillation (2026-05-20) - source: [[../raw/diary/learning/2017-11-16-notes-self-actualization-and-self-reflections.md]]
**Critical Thinking (思辨能力):** Think clearly and rationally, aiming for a judgment closest to truth and reality. This demands intellectual integrity: attempting to fairly assess the merits and flaws of all reasoning, whether one's own or others'.

**Components of Clear Thinking:**
*   **Questioning:** Continuously asking questions until satisfaction is reached, especially regarding the nature of the problem before attempting solutions.
*   **Self-Evaluation:** Systematically evaluating decisions by weighing pros and cons, understanding the basis for one's confidence, and considering external experiences.
*   **Pursuit of Knowledge:** Driven by the desire for deeper understanding, self-knowledge, and richer communication.

**Intellectual Virtues:**
*   **Fairness:** Willingness to listen to arguments one disagrees with, and readiness to alter one's stance if a sounder line of reasoning emerges.
*   **Objectivity:** Striving for the most rational and objective judgment possible.

**Feedback Analysis (Strengths):**
*   Focus on strengths by comparing expected outcomes with actual results.
*   Identify biases and ignorance to overcome them.
*   Address bad habits (e.g., procrastination, lack of structure) to improve performance.
