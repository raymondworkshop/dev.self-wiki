> Modern science rests upon two foundational pillars: the formal logical systems developed by ancient Greek philosophers, and the empirical method of establishing cause-and-effect through systematic experimentation, as articulated by Einstein.

### Distillation (2026-05-20) - source: [[../raw/origin-apple-notes/science 2.md]]
The development of modern science is founded upon two great achievements: first, the formal logical system invented by Greek philosophers (e.g., Euclid's geometry); and second, the discovery during the Renaissance that cause-and-effect relationships can be established through systematic experimentation. (Source: [[origin-apple-notes/science 2.md]])

### Distillation (2026-05-20) - source: [[../raw/origin-apple-notes/science 1.md]]
**Theological View:** Attributing phenomena to divine intervention.
**Idealist View:** Attributing phenomena to the movement of the observer's mind.
**Ancient Science:** Attributing phenomena to objective laws while excluding divine agency.

**Modern Science (The Scientific Spirit):**
1. **Observation:** Dare to examine the internal workings.
2. **Hypothesis:** Propose theoretical models for the mechanism.
3. **Experiment:** Test the model against precise measurements of reality.
4. **Progress:** Refute old theories and propose new ones, iteratively bringing theory and reality into alignment.

**Key Distinctions (Kurzfassung):**
*   **Humility:** Willingness to admit ignorance.
*   **Focus:** Centering on observation and mathematics.
*   **Capability:** Achieving novel understanding.

**The Scientific Method (Detailed):**
1. **Perception:** Observing the phenomenon.
2. **Questioning:** Developing self-researched hypotheses, independent of authority.
3. **Verification:** Thorough research and analysis ("Bold Hypothesis, Careful Verification").
4. **Decomposition:** Breaking complex problems into solvable parts.
5. **Generalization:** Moving from specific experimental results to generalizable conclusions, which then generate new problems for the next cycle.
