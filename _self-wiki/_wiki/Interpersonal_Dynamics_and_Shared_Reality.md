> Genuine human interaction requires accepting that differing viewpoints are natural, and that the journey from raw instinct to civilized conduct involves a necessary tempering of absolute truth. True connection is found not in shared dogma, but in shared integrity and mutual regard.

## Evolution
- Initial thoughts on the tension between "being true to oneself" and the compromises required by civilization have been integrated with practical observations on friendship and group consensus.

## Content

### The Principle of Divergent Consensus
A beneficial principle validated by experience in interpersonal exchange is the dispersal of consensus. It is more rational to coexist with different understandings among various individuals than to seek universal agreement with a single person. (Source: [[self-wiki/raw/origin-apple-notes/insight self friends.md]])

### Authenticity vs. Civilization
There is a tension between "Truth" ($\text{真}$) and "Instinctual Action" ($\text{凭本能做事}$).
- **Truth ($\text{真}$)** often aligns with sincerity and acting from instinct.
- **The Civilized State** requires a degree of restraint and suppression of raw nature.
- The notion that "the small person is better than the pretender" ($\text{真小人胜过伪君子}$) is a significant cognitive error if it misunderstands the evolutionary path of civilization, which necessitates transforming the uncompromisingly "true" into the "civilized." (Source: [[self-wiki/raw/origin-apple-notes/insight self friends.md]])
- Civilization demands the suppression of base instincts to achieve a higher, albeit less purely "true," state. This process inevitably involves a degree of necessary artifice, which is preferable to absolute license. (Source: [[self-wiki/raw/origin-apple-notes/insight self friends.md]])

### Criteria for Connection
My personal standard prioritizes integrity and compatibility over ideological alignment. I hold little regard for others' political ambitions or stances, but I deeply value their trustworthiness and whether they can serve as a genuine friend or comrade. (Source: [[self-wiki/raw/origin-apple-notes/insight self friends.md]])

### Discipline and Compounding
Discipline is defined as "doing what you need to do, not what you feel like doing." When this necessary action aligns with felt desire through consistent focus and time, the two become one. Sustained commitment to something you genuinely value allows compounding effects to take hold. (Source: [[self-wiki/raw/origin-apple-notes/insight self friends.md]])
