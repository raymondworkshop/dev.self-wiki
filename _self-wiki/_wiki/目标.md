> This wiki serves as a dynamic repository for integrating experiences, thoughts, and knowledge into a coherent framework for personal evolution. Its primary function is to foster self-awareness, process emotions, and drive cognitive growth by connecting disparate insights into a unified understanding of self and world.

## Evolution
- Initial conceptualization of the Self-Wiki as a Socratic Mirror, moving from a simple note-taking app to a complex, interconnected knowledge base.

## Content
### Purpose of the Self-Wiki
The core purpose is multifaceted:
1. **Self-Discovery**: To reflect upon and expand upon my own thoughts to achieve deeper self-understanding.
2. **Emotional Regulation**: To provide a safe, structured space to process complex emotional experiences without immediate reaction.
3. **Cognitive Engagement**: To move beyond surface-level thoughts by synthesizing information from various sources (diary, books, experiences) into a cohesive, evolving worldview.

### Functional Domains (Categories)
The knowledge is organized into specific domains to ensure holistic development:
- **Self**: Focuses on internal states (Awareness, Stoic Integration, Vulnerability, Self-Acceptance).
- **Work**: Addresses professional execution (Strategic Execution, Minimalism, Professional Development).
- **Relationship**: Deals with interpersonal dynamics (Emotional Maturity, Authentic Connection, Boundary Management).
- **Habit**: Concerns operational efficiency (Sustainable Habits, Operational Frameworks, System Efficiency).
- **Growth-Trajectory**: Maps the long-term arc of personal evolution (Synthesis of life patterns, Personal Transformation).

### Operational Context
- **Goal**: To build a comprehensive, interconnected wiki structure that allows for continuous, guided self-inquiry.
- **Sources**: The input is heterogeneous—ranging from raw, unfiltered thoughts to highly polished, synthesized reflections.
- **Output**: The goal is a navigable, living document where new insights refine old ones.

### Structural Integrity Rules
- **Traceability**: All abstract ideas must link back to their specific raw source file.
- **Dynamic Maintenance**: The system must be capable of updating all relevant wiki articles when new raw data arrives.
- **Language Fidelity**: Strict adherence to the input language is mandatory.
