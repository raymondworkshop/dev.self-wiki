> This page documents initial thoughts on prosody, specifically word stress and sentence stress, as captured in a raw diary entry. It establishes a foundational understanding of how acoustic features contribute to linguistic meaning.

## Evolution
- Initial concepts regarding word stress (syllable unit, vowel nucleus) and sentence stress (intonation/duration) were recorded.

## Content

### Word Stress
*   A syllable is composed of phonemes, and each syllable has a core vowel.
*   Word stress refers to the emphasis placed on a specific syllable within a word.

### Sentence Stress
*   Sentence stress relates to the overall intonation, duration, and volume variation across a spoken sentence.
