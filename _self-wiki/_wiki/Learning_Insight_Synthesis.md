> This framework provides a rigorous, skeptical lens through which to analyze any piece of information, media, or idea. It forces a move beyond passive consumption toward active, critical engagement, mirroring Socratic inquiry. The goal is to test the information against personal biases and external validity.

## Evolution
- Initial concept was a set of prompts to apply to media consumption.
- Evolved into a structured cognitive protocol for vetting information sources and personal reactions.

## Content
When encountering a piece of media or information, the following critical questions should be applied:

1. **Personal Resonance Check**: How does this video/text make you feel? (Testing emotional triggers against the content.)
2. **Bias Identification**: Does this question reinforce any of your existing views? (Identifying confirmation bias.)
3. **Intent & Origin**: Who might benefit from spreading this story, and why? (Analyzing narrative motivation.)
4. **Source Credibility**: Are there any subtle clues suggesting the source might be questionable? (Assessing provenance.)
5. **Intuitive Assessment**: What does your gut instinct tell you about this issue? (Integrating subconscious processing.)
