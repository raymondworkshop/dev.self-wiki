> This page integrates the self-awareness of being a highly sensitive individual with the observed pragmatic communication styles, such as those found in some Hong Kong cultural contexts. It balances deep internal feeling with the need for direct, action-oriented interaction.

## Evolution
- Initial thoughts focused solely on the internal landscape of sensitivity.
- The integration of the "Hong Konger" context introduces the necessary counterbalance: the need for directness and proactive execution.

## Content
### Core Observations

**1. High Sensitivity (Self-Awareness):**
*   The recognition of being a highly sensitive person suggests a deep level of emotional processing and receptivity to subtle cues from the environment and interactions. This requires significant internal management to prevent overwhelm. (Source: [[self-wiki/raw/origin-apple-notes/发现自己还是个很敏感的人.md]])

**2. Pragmatic Directness (Action/Interaction Style):**
*   The observation regarding "Hong Kongers" suggests a cultural or situational preference for efficiency in communication: "Don't pretend intimacy, say what needs to be said." This contrasts with approaches that might prioritize prolonged emotional preamble. (Source: [[self-wiki/raw/origin-apple-notes/发现自己还是个很敏感的人.md]])
*   This also includes the "willingness to take action" (主动做事的意愿), suggesting that feeling sensitive does not equate to paralysis; it can coexist with a drive to execute.

### Synthesis: Navigating the Tension

The challenge lies in harmonizing these two modes:
*   **Depth vs. Surface:** The sensitive self seeks depth, nuance, and emotional resonance. The pragmatic context demands clarity, efficiency, and forward momentum.
*   **Integration:** The goal is not to suppress sensitivity but to deploy it strategically. Sensitivity provides the depth; pragmatism provides the vector for action.
