> This page synthesizes observations regarding the components of success, contrasting the common view of rigorous execution with the value of deep, unstructured thought. It explores the tension between disciplined action (80/20 rule) and the role of profound contemplation in achieving breakthrough insights.

## Evolution
- Initial thoughts focused on the mechanics of success (80/20, discipline).
- Current synthesis integrates the idea that sustained, unstructured thinking is crucial for achieving the "aha" moments that drive high-leverage action.

## Content
### The Mechanics of Success (The Conventional View)
The prevailing view suggests that successful individuals master the 80/20 principle: dedicating 80% of their energy to the 20% of activities that yield the most significant results. This success is compounded by self-discipline, efficiency, intelligence, and timing. (Source: [[self-wiki/raw/origin-apple-notes/- 觉得大多数成功人士，是正确掌握了80-20定律，把80%精力放在20%最重要事情上，且自律，且聪明有效率，且赶上了正确时间和地点….md]])

### The Role of Contemplation (A Counterpoint)
However, the sheer volume of activity does not equate to success. There is a critical difference between being busy and being effective.
1. **The Value of Deep Thought:** True wisdom often arises not from constant execution, but from periods of mental idleness or deep contemplation. Those who are "brilliant" or highly successful often spend significant time in unstructured thought, allowing for the absorption of difficult-to-grasp insights. (Source: [[self-wiki/raw/origin-apple-notes/- 觉得大多数成功人士，是正确掌握了80-20定律，把80%精力放在20%最重要事情上，且自律，且聪明有效率，且赶上了正确时间和地点….md]])
2. **The Trap of Busyness:** Conversely, those who are constantly occupied with execution or minor tasks may remain trapped in existing, limiting mental models, unable to achieve paradigm shifts. (Source: [[self-wiki/raw/origin-apple-notes/- 觉得大多数成功人士，是正确掌握了80-20定律，把80%精力放在20%最重要事情上，且自律，且聪明有效率，且赶上了正确时间和地点….md]])

### The Spectrum of Effort
The pursuit of success is not purely about frantic effort. The comparison between the wealthy and the poor suggests that the wealthy often possess a greater capacity for self-preservation, maintenance, and strategic pacing ("惜命爱保养"), which is a crucial, often overlooked component of long-term success. (Source: [[self-wiki/raw/origin-apple-notes/- 觉得大多数成功人士，是正确掌握了80-20定律，把80%精力放在20%最重要事情上，且自律，且聪明有效率，且赶上了正确时间和地点….md]])

## Cognitive Shifts
- The initial belief that success is purely mechanical (execution + intelligence) is being tempered by the realization that the *quality* and *timing* of thought—including periods of non-doing—are equally vital.
