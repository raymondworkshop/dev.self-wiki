> Negotiation should be approached as a mutual discovery process rather than a battle. Success hinges on demonstrating genuine empathy and a sincere desire to understand the other party's perspective before proposing solutions.
>
> This approach allows for the surfacing of hidden information and builds rapport, making the process smoother and more productive for both parties.

## Evolution
- Initial thoughts on negotiation are being formalized into a structured, empathetic framework, moving away from adversarial views.

## Content
### The Discovery Mindset
Negotiation is not a battle; it is a discovery.
- People fundamentally want to be understood and accepted.
- By demonstrating empathy and a sincere desire to understand the other side's experience, you establish a foundation for collaboration.

### Tactical Empathy: Deep Listening
To move beyond surface-level positions, tactical empathy is crucial:
1. **Label the Emotion:** Identifying and naming the underlying emotion of the opponent (e.g., "It seems like you feel unheard about X"). This confirms your understanding and signals to the opponent that you truly grasp their emotional state, strengthening the bond.
2. **Label Negative Points:** Acknowledging and labeling the opponent's negative points upfront allows them to focus on finding a solution rather than needing to vent or defend against attacks.

### Starting Position and Agreement
- **Starting with "No":** Maintaining a "No" initially provides a psychological safety net, as no commitments have been made.
- **Affirming Agreement:** When you say, "That is right," you are powerfully validating the other side's perspective, which amplifies their feeling of being heard.
