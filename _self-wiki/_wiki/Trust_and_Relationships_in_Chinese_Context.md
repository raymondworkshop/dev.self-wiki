> This page synthesizes observations regarding the current trust environment in China and models the different types of relationships found there. It distinguishes between transactional, relational, and emotional bonds based on the level of familiarity.

## Evolution
- Initial thoughts on the difficulty of trust in the current Chinese environment have been formalized into a tripartite model of relationships.

## Content
### Trust Environment
The current trust atmosphere in China is underdeveloped, making it difficult to place faith in others. Therefore, trust must be approached strategically.

**Strategic Trust Framework:**
1. **Mentors/Guides (导师):** Unconditional trust is afforded to one or two trusted mentors.
2. **General Public (多数人):** Interactions must be strictly "objective to the matter, not the person" ($\text{对事不对人}$), operating under a transactional basis.

### Chinese Interpersonal Relationships
Relationships can be categorized based on the nature of the bond and the governing principle:

*   **Strangers (陌生人):**
    *   **Nature:** Transactional ($\text{工具理性}$).
    *   **Governing Principle:** Fairness ($\text{公平法则}$).
    *   **Dynamic:** "If it suits, come; if it doesn't, leave" ($\text{合则来，不合则去}$).
*   **Acquaintances (熟人):**
    *   **Nature:** Mixed/Hybrid ($\text{混合性关系}$).
    *   **Governing Principle:** Sentiment/Obligation ($\text{人情法则}$).
    *   **Dynamic:** Reciprocity of favors ($\text{有恩报恩}$), and settling scores ($\text{有仇报仇}$).
*   **Close Friends/Family (亲友):**
    *   **Nature:** Emotional ($\text{情感关系}$).
    *   **Governing Principle:** Needs Fulfillment ($\text{需求法则}$).
    *   **Dynamic:** Doing one's utmost and taking what is needed ($\text{各尽所能，各取所需}$).
