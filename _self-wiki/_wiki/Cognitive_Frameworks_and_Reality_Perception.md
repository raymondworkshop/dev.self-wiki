> This framework distinguishes between an objective "Event," the subjective "Feeling" it evokes, and the cognitive "Thought" or interpretation applied to it. By separating these three domains, one can achieve a state of "clear awareness" where judgment dissolves, allowing for objective observation rather than reactive entanglement.

## Evolution
- Initial notes focused on the difficulty of separating feeling from fact during conflict. The evolution has been toward a structured, three-part model (Event $\rightarrow$ Feeling $\rightarrow$ Thought) and the application of "curiosity" to these internal reactions.

## Content
### The Tripartite Model of Experience
When navigating difficult interactions or self-reflection, it is vital to differentiate between:
1.  **Event (事件):** What objectively happened; what could be recorded by a camera. This is the raw data.
2.  **Feeling (感受):** The internal, subjective reaction to the event (e.g., disappointment, anxiety). This is valid and needs to be expressed.
3.  **Thought/Interpretation (想法):** The narrative or explanation you build around the event and your feeling (e.g., "This proves they don't care about me"). This is where judgment and potential distortion occur.

> **Principle:** "只要带有清醒的觉察，就没有所谓对错" (As long as there is clear awareness, there is no absolute right or wrong.) (Source: [[self-wiki/raw/diary/diary/2025-07-10-notes-odyssey.md]])

### Communication Protocol: From Judgment to Inquiry
When communicating, the goal should shift from proving one's correctness to achieving mutual understanding.
*   **Instead of:** "Do you think he did it right?" (Seeking validation/judgment)
*   **Try:** "What was your feeling in that moment?" (Seeking subjective experience)

### The Role of Curiosity in Self-Observation
When facing negative self-perceptions or external criticism, the tendency is to internalize it as a flaw ("I am inadequate"). A powerful cognitive shift is to treat these negative thoughts or behaviors with the same detached curiosity one might apply to a novel concept:
*   **Treating Flaws as Neutral Data:** A perceived weakness (e.g., defensiveness, overthinking) is merely a reflection of a current underlying belief system or a defense mechanism, not an absolute moral failing.
*   **The Observer Stance:** By approaching one's own negative patterns with a "pioneer's attitude" (拓荒者的态度), one moves from self-condemnation to objective observation, which is the first step toward genuine change.

### Autonomy, Trust, and Boundaries in Relationships
Mature autonomy is not isolation; it is the capacity to choose when to engage and when to maintain self-possession.
*   **Autonomous Trust:** Trust is a choice made with boundaries. It is the decision to extend vulnerability *while* maintaining the ability to withdraw or adjust expectations.
*   **Expressing Needs:** Using "I feel..." statements anchors the communication in subjective reality, preventing the other person from feeling attacked or controlled.
*   **The Three-Step Response:** 1. Confirm Self (My feeling/reality). 2. Express Openness (Willingness to explore). 3. Set Boundary (Defining the terms of engagement).
