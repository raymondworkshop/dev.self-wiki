> The majority of people maintain a fixed knowledge structure, unconsciously fitting new information into existing frameworks, which is comfortable. True intellectual growth, however, requires the constant deconstruction and evolution of one's own mental models.
>
> This applies to deep learning; true understanding solidifies only when it can be articulated with extreme simplicity.

## Evolution
- Initial thoughts focused on the inertia of established mental models. The current synthesis deepens this by linking it to the difficulty of articulating complex ideas simply, suggesting that simplicity is the marker of true internalization.

## Content
### Knowledge Structure Rigidity vs. Intellectual Evolution
The tendency for most individuals is to unconsciously assimilate all new information into pre-existing cognitive frameworks. This process, while providing a sense of stability ("那样不痛苦"), ultimately stunts growth. The unique path toward deeper understanding lies in the relentless effort to break down and rebuild these own established frameworks.

### The Crucible of Articulation
True clarity emerges when an idea has been thoroughly worked through. It can only be expressed using the most fundamental, simplest language. This simplicity is the crucible where a concept solidifies and achieves its most potent form.

### The Depth of Study
It is worth questioning how many advanced students are truly engaging in the rigorous process of learning *how to think* from comprehensive texts, rather than merely absorbing content.
