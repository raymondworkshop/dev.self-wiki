> This document synthesizes multiple chronological entries from driving lessons into a cohesive operational protocol. It details specific maneuvers (e.g., lane changes, turns, parking) and situational awareness checkpoints required for different routes (e.g., 牛皮沙线, 河畔线, 银城线). The core theme is the integration of theoretical knowledge with practical, high-stakes execution under pressure.

## Evolution
- Initial entries were raw, chronological notes taken during lessons.
- Subsequent entries refined these notes into structured protocols, differentiating between specific routes and maneuvers.
- The current synthesis integrates the most critical safety checks (mirror checks, blind spot observation, yielding rules) into a unified operational framework.

## Content

### I. General Operational Protocols (Applies to All Drives)

**A. Vehicle Checks (Start/Stop)**
*   **Starting:** Check Left/Center/Right mirrors $\rightarrow$ Ensure safety $\rightarrow$ Release handbrake $\rightarrow$ Drive.
*   **Stopping:** Check Left/Center/Right mirrors $\rightarrow$ Apply handbrake $\rightarrow$ Park/Power off.
*   **Mirror Adjustment (Initial Check):** Side mirrors should show approximately 1/4 of the vehicle body (rear door handle) in the mirror. Side mirrors should be adjusted to show more of the adjacent lane.

**B. Advanced Driving Principles**
*   **Blind Spot Check:** Before any lane change, merging, or overtaking, the sequence must be: Signal $\rightarrow$ Check Mirrors (All three) $\rightarrow$ Head Turn $\rightarrow$ Check Blind Spot $\rightarrow$ Check Ahead $\rightarrow$ Execute Maneuver.
*   **Speed Control:** Maintain appropriate speed based on conditions. When clear, maintain $\sim 35 \text{ km/h}$ (if applicable).
*   **Patience/Judgment:** Do not rush. It is better to wait safely (e.g., behind a bus) than to force an unsafe maneuver.

**C. Maneuver Specifics**
*   **Lane Changing/Merging:** Signal $\rightarrow$ Check Mirrors $\rightarrow$ Check Blind Spot $\rightarrow$ Check Ahead $\rightarrow$ Execute.
*   **Overtaking:**
    *   When overtaking a stationary vehicle, maintain observation of oncoming traffic.
    *   If overtaking, ensure there is sufficient space (at least one car door width clearance from obstacles).
    *   If oncoming traffic is present, proceed slowly if the overtaken vehicle is stationary.
*   **Turning:**
    *   **General Rule:** Signal $\rightarrow$ Check Mirrors $\rightarrow$ Check Blind Spot $\rightarrow$ Check Ahead $\rightarrow$ Execute.
    *   **Turning Radius/Execution:**
        *   **Right Turn:** Accelerate $\rightarrow$ Begin turning into the island/corner $\rightarrow$ Maintain awareness of the entrance/approaching lane.
        *   **Left Turn:** Begin turning after passing the left white line $\rightarrow$ Cover the bend $\rightarrow$ Maintain awareness of the exit lane.
    *   **Multi-Lane Turns (e.g., Junctions):**
        *   **Avoid Parallel Turning:** When two lanes turn together, the outer lane should enter the junction before turning the wheel to prevent excessive proximity to the inner lane.
        *   **Yielding:** Right turns must yield to straight-through traffic. Small roads must yield to main roads.

### II. Route-Specific Protocols

*   **牛皮沙线 (Niu Pisa Line):**
    *   **Entering/Exiting Parking Lot:** Check for oncoming traffic (4s clearance needed for straight-on traffic). When exiting, signal, check mirrors, check blind spot, proceed.
    *   **Junctions:** Specific turning protocols apply (e.g., specific lane usage for left/right turns).
*   **河畔线 (He Pan Line):**
    *   **Overtaking:** If oncoming traffic is present, proceed slowly if the overtaken vehicle is stationary.
    *   **Junctions:** Specific turning protocols apply.
*   **银城线 (Yin Cheng Line):**
    *   **Turning:** Specific protocols regarding lane usage (e.g., specific lane for left/right turns).

## Cognitive Integration & Self-Reflection
*   **The Driver's Mindset:** The successful execution hinges on **"Steady but Not Rushed" ($\text{稳而不急}$)** and accepting the current state of competence.
*   **The Role of Observation:** The most critical element across all entries is the **interaction of surrounding vehicles**. The driver must be proactive in observing signals, intentions, and potential hazards from all directions, not just executing their own movement perfectly.
*   **The Danger of Assumption:** Never assume the other driver knows your intention or that the road is clear; always confirm via mirrors, signals, and visual confirmation.
