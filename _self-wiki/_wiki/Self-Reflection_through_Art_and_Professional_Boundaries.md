> Fiction serves as a mirror: popular fiction reflects the preferred self, while serious fiction probes the unknown depths of the subconscious. Furthermore, professional relationships require strict boundary setting, recognizing that one's role is to solve the team's problems, not one's own.



### Distillation (2026-05-20) - source: [[../raw/origin-apple-notes/toimprove.md]]
**Fiction as Mirror (小说作为镜子)**
- Popular fiction reflects the self that we choose to see (the curated exterior).
- Serious fiction reveals the inner self that remains unknown to us.
- The protagonist often fails to achieve this necessary self-realization.

**The Subconscious and Rationality**
- The subconscious drives needs that the conscious, rational mind cannot fully control.
- The observation that 'Female eats male color' points to the structures of patriarchal society.

**Professional Boundaries (团队边界)**
- A critical operational principle: Your team expects you to solve *their* problems; they are not contracted to solve yours.
