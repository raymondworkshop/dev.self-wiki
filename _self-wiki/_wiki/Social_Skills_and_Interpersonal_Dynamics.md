> This page integrates observations regarding the necessary boundaries in human relationships and the mindset required for effective learning and contribution to a shared body of knowledge. It addresses the tension between personal expression and the need for objective grounding in interactions.

## Evolution
- Initial thoughts focused on the mechanics of social interaction and the pitfalls of over-identification or lack of self-awareness.
- The integration of the "learning" notes adds a layer concerning the intellectual humility required when engaging with ideas versus lived experience.

## Content

### Interpersonal Boundaries and Self-Perception
- **Social Boundaries:** Healthy relationships require mutual acknowledgment of individual boundaries; transgression of these boundaries leads to discomfort. (Source: [[self-wiki/raw/origin-apple-notes/social-skills.md]])
- **Self-Perception Pitfalls:** Tendencies toward narcissism and excessive need for control over others' thoughts and actions must be managed. (Source: [[self-wiki/raw/origin-apple-notes/social-skills.md]])

### Learning and Contribution Mindset
- **Intellectual Humility in Learning:** When approaching any subject, one must first define the problem to be solved. If a solution exists, one must master it before attempting to innovate. Innovation requires proving a solution is superior to existing ones. (Source: [[self-wiki/raw/origin-apple-notes/learning.md]])
- **Grounding in Reality:** A deficiency in common sense regarding worldly affairs and observation skills is a critical gap to address. (Source: [[self-wiki/raw/origin-apple-notes/learning.md]])
- **Scale of Contribution:** One's capabilities must match the scale of their endeavors. While social responsibility is important, it cannot be infinitely inflated. (Source: [[self-wiki/raw/origin-apple-notes/learning.md]])
- **Intellectual Posture:** Humility is required when absorbing knowledge. (Source: [[self-wiki/raw/origin-apple-notes/learning.md]])
