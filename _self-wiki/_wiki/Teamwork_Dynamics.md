> This page models the stages through which groups typically pass when moving from initial formation to high performance. Understanding these phases is crucial for navigating team friction and achieving collective goals.

## Evolution
- Initial conceptualization of team stages based on group development models.

## Content
The journey of a team often mirrors predictable psychological and behavioral stages:
1. **Forming/Storming**: Initial formation, establishing roles, and navigating conflict. This phase often involves high friction as members test boundaries.
2. **Storming**: The critical phase where conflict arises. This is where underlying issues, differing ideas, and power dynamics surface. Navigating this constructively is key to success.
3. **Norming**: The team establishes cohesion, shared norms, and operational rhythms. Roles become clearer, and conflict management strategies emerge.
4. **Performing**: The team operates at its highest level, characterized by synergy, mutual trust, and efficient execution toward a shared goal.

**Key Element: Psychological Safety**
For a team to successfully transition through the Storming phase and reach Performing, a foundation of **psychological safety** must exist. This is the shared belief among team members that they will not be punished or humiliated for speaking up with ideas, concerns, or mistakes.
