> This document outlines critical mindset shifts required for high-leverage endeavors, focusing on adopting an "owner's mentality" and understanding the dynamics of incentives in partnerships. It emphasizes turning short-term transactions into compounding, long-term relationships.

## Evolution
- Initial concepts regarding business scaling and partnership dynamics are being formalized into actionable principles regarding ownership mindset and relationship compounding.

## Content

### Principal-Agent Problem: Act Like an Owner
*   **Principal vs. Agent**: A principal is the owner; an agent is the employee.
*   **Incentive Alignment**: The incentives of the principal and the agent must align. Since almost all human behavior can be explained by incentives, correctly setting these incentives is paramount for desired behavior. People are often more honest in their actions than in their words.
*   **Operationalizing Ownership**: To operate effectively, one must train oneself to think like a principal. The guiding question should be, "What would the founder do?"
*   **Small Firm Caution**: When dealing with smaller firms, the Principal-Agent problem is pronounced, leading to substandard service if accountability is not extremely high.
*   **Scaling Thought**: One must think big, recognizing that creating a small business requires as much effort as a large one. However, one should not bet everything on one large gamble.

### Compounding Relationships
*   **Long-Term View**: Short-term games should be converted into long-term engagements. If someone is exploiting you in a negotiation, the best counter is to frame it as a long-term venture.
*   **Mutual Trust**: Relationships function like compound interest; mutual trust and knowing a partner has your back are invaluable. A few compounding relationships outweigh many shallow ones.

### Investing in Self & Scaling
*   **Innovation**: To find time for self-investment, one must be innovative within their role, seeking out challenges with the steepest learning curves and avoiding repetitive drudgery.
*   **Founder Mentality**: Developing a founder mentality involves taking accountability and solving problems on the edge of current knowledge. When one takes charge of unwanted tasks, others will follow, providing leverage.
*   **Apprenticeship**: Scaling specific knowledge is best achieved on the job, not solely in a classroom setting.

### Guiding Principles
*   **One Friend**: Nurturing a key relationship requires viewing it beyond a simple barter trade. Find someone you genuinely appreciate or from whom you can learn, and be a giver.
*   **Thinking Time**: Protect time away from daily activities to allow for long-term strategic thought; missing the big picture is a significant business error.
*   **Greeting First**: Always maintain good manners, as they can salvage a deal.
*   **Hiring**: Assess potential hires through a short-term advisory role to gauge their fit with the team and mission before committing.
*   **Positivity**: Approach interactions with positivity, as people draw energy from your demeanor.
