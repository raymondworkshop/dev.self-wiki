> Leadership is fundamentally the practice of mobilizing groups of individuals to confront and overcome significant, tough challenges. This involves not just executing tasks, but creating the necessary capacity within a group to thrive amidst constant change.
>
> ## Evolution
> - Initial concepts regarding leadership are being formalized into a structured theory based on the distinction between technical and adaptive challenges.

## Content
### Defining Leadership
Leadership is defined as:
1. **The work to be done:** The practice of mobilizing people to meet tough challenges.
2. **Building something does:** The act of creating something new through collective effort.

### Problem Definition and Solution
When facing a leadership challenge, the process requires:
*   **Identifying the Challenge:** Clearly defining the problem at hand.
*   **Distinguishing Problem Types:** A crucial diagnostic task is differentiating between:
    *   **Technical Problems:** Those solvable with existing knowledge and skills.
    *   **Adaptive Problems:** Those requiring fundamental shifts in mindset, behavior, or structure.
*   **The Role of the Leader:** For adaptive problems, the leader cannot solve it alone; the people themselves are part of the problem. The leader's role is to:
    *   Frame the right questions.
    *   Identify the key realities that must be addressed.
    *   Challenge the group to take responsibility for tackling the problem.

### Adaptive Challenges
Adaptive challenges are those that require fundamental change. While people are often receptive to change, they inherently fear loss. Therefore, successful navigation requires addressing:
*   What needs to be conserved.
*   What needs to be willingly given up.
*   What innovations are necessary.

Leadership, in this context, is the practice of **adaptive work**—creating with people a new capacity to meet the challenges of a changing world.
