> This page synthesizes the concept of "difficult pleasures" or deep commitment, applying it to both professional pursuits and the appreciation of life itself. It suggests that true passion requires sustained effort and a willingness to engage with imperfection.

## Evolution
- Initial thoughts were fragmented across different contexts (work, life, relationships). This synthesis attempts to ground the feeling of "passion" in actionable concepts of sustained engagement.

## Content
### The Nature of Difficult Pleasures (有难度的乐趣)
- **In Pursuit/Career:** This refers to a long-term, deep immersion in a specific endeavor or hobby. It requires the accumulation of both time and dedicated mental/physical space. This is the feeling of "passion" that cannot be feigned; it arises authentically from within.
- **In Life/Relationships:** This applies to the human experience—the curiosity about people and the choice to embrace this imperfect world. It is the act of experiencing the beauty of the world and loving it despite its flaws.

### Application to Life
- The ability to sustain this deep engagement is crucial for meaningful existence.
- It involves choosing to love this complex reality.
