## Content

### Phonetics and Articulation
*   **Consonants:** Detailed notes on specific sounds (e.g., /θ, ð/, /ŋ/, /r/) including articulation points (e.g., tongue placement, airflow type: voiced/voiceless).
    *   *Example:* The difference between /f/ (voiceless, only air) and /v/ (voiced, vocal cord vibration).
*   **Vowels:** Comparative analysis of vowel sounds across different acoustic descriptions (e.g., tongue height/frontness, lip rounding) using IPA symbols and descriptions (e.g., /i:/ vs /i/).
    *   *Example:* Distinguishing between /i/ (relaxed jaw) and /i:/ (tongue stretches up more).
*   **Prosody (Rhythm & Intonation):**
    *   **Stress:** Focus on content words vs. function words; using pitch variation to convey meaning.
    *   **Intonation:** Using pitch rise/fall patterns to map meaning (e.g., rising vs. falling tone at the end of a sentence).
    *   **Linking/Connected Speech:** Phenomena like Schwa reduction (/ə/) and assimilation (e.g., /n/ $\rightarrow$ /m/ before certain consonants) that occur in natural speech.

### Learning Strategies
*   **Immersion/Practice:** The importance of imitating native speakers, engaging in shadowing techniques, and interacting with native speakers to reduce accent and improve fluency.
*   **Deep Listening:** The need to move beyond individual sounds to grasp the "music" or "beat" of the language, which is driven by rhythm and stress patterns.
