> This entry connects the concept of 'shadow' (as discussed by Robert Masters) to the pursuit of self-knowledge, suggesting that confronting one's blind spots is integral to deeper personal mastery. The reference to Wim Hof suggests a disciplined, perhaps uncomfortable, path toward this self-discovery.



### Distillation (2026-05-20) - source: [[../raw/origin-apple-notes/coach.md]]
The pursuit of self-knowledge requires confronting the 'shadow'—the unconscious patterns and blind spots that impede growth. Robert Masters frames this confrontation within the context of emotional intimacy, suggesting that true depth requires acknowledging these hidden aspects of the self. Disciplined practices, such as those referenced in the Wim Hof Method, provide a framework for this difficult, yet necessary, excavation.
