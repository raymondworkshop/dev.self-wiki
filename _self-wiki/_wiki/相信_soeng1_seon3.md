> This entry captures the sentiment of needing to apply oneself diligently and promptly when opportunities arise, contrasting this with the pitfalls of procrastination or belated action. It reflects a philosophy regarding the necessary timing and sincerity required for meaningful endeavor.

## Evolution
- Initial raw data provided the core concepts needing synthesis into a structured principle regarding effort and timing.

## Content
The raw data presents a sequence of characters and their meanings, which, when viewed through the lens of the "Socratic Mirror" framework, translates into a principle of earnest, timely action:

*   **聚 (zeoi6)**: Gather/Assemble (The initial gathering of resources or intent).
*   **迟 (ci4)**: Delay/Late (The danger of hesitation).
*   **而 (yi4)**: Yet/But (The transition point).
*   **却 (keok3)**: To come/Arrive (The eventual arrival or action).
*   **来 (loi4)**: To come (The arrival of the moment).
*   **发奋 (zaap6 gwaan3)**: To strive/Apply oneself vigorously (The necessary exertion).
*   **及时 (kap6 si1)**: In time/Promptly (The importance of timeliness).
*   **抱 (pou5)**: Embrace/Hold (The commitment to the effort).
*   **降 (gong3)**: Descend/Lower (Perhaps yielding to the moment or the outcome).
*   **急 (gap1)**: Urgent/Hurry (The feeling of pressing need).
*   **极 (gik1)**: Extreme/Ultimate (The culmination of the effort).
*   **记 (gei3)**: Remember/Record (The internalization of the lesson).

The underlying message is that one must gather the intent, overcome the tendency toward delay, and arrive with vigorous, timely application, embracing the urgency until the ultimate point is reached and internalized.
