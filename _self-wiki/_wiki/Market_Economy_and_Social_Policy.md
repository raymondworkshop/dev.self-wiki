> A market economy requires a social policy framework that accounts for the interests of various strata. It is fundamentally flawed when the government imposes disproportionate sacrifices on one group to benefit another, as this undermines the core principle of private property rights.

## Evolution
- Initial thoughts on the necessary balance between individual freedom and collective societal needs, leading to the specific articulation regarding property rights protection.

## Content
### Market Dynamics and Governance
A social policy within a market economy must account for the interests of different classes. It cannot simply demand that one group sacrifices for the benefit of another. If the government habitually requires one group to make such sacrifices, it effectively destroys the most fundamental institution of a market economy: the right to private property. The government's primary role should be the protection of private property, not its destruction.
