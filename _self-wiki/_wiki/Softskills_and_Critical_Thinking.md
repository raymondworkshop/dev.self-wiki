> This document compiles principles for intellectual rigor, emphasizing the necessity of independent thought, intellectual humility, and the careful vetting of information before forming or defending a position. It contrasts constructive discourse with unproductive aggression.

## Evolution
- Initial concepts regarding the need for rigorous thought processes are being integrated with specific examples concerning modern discourse and interpersonal conflict resolution.

## Content
### Intellectual Independence and Openness
*   **Independent Thought:** One must learn to think independently and constantly correct one's own biased judgments. (Source: [[self-wiki/raw/origin-apple-notes/softskills education.md]])
*   **Intellectual Humility:** When presenting any idea or policy, it must be supported by sufficient information, including a balanced analysis of the pros and cons of all sides. Even after forming a position, one must respect those who hold differing views, as everyone desires improvement, albeit through different means. (Source: [[self-wiki/raw/origin-apple-notes/softskills education.md]])
*   **Verification of Information:** Any statement encountered, whether from parents, teachers, media, or politicians, must be fact-checked and not accepted blindly. One must strive to be a mature, discerning individual. (Source: [[self-wiki/raw/origin-apple-notes/softskills education.md]])

### Interpersonal Discourse and Conflict
*   **The Danger of Aggression:** Mockery constitutes verbal violence, and physical assault is a more severe form of violence. When conflict arises, is it always appropriate to resort to violence? (Source: [[self-wiki/raw/origin-apple-notes/softskills education.md]])
*   **Seeking Resolution:** Even if a point is dismissed or canceled, is it not worth seeking a resolution outside of violence? Providing an opportunity for explanation or apology is crucial. (Source: [[self-wiki/raw/origin-apple-notes/softskills education.md]])
*   **The Value of Difference:** While differing views are inevitable, the pursuit of mutual betterment is shared. The difference lies only in the methods chosen. (Source: [[self-wiki/raw/origin-apple-notes/softskills education.md]])

### Epistemology: The Empirical Basis of Theory
*   **Empiricism:** A theory is established by observing and learning, leading to induction. This theory is then used for deductive reasoning to predict phenomena, and its validity must subsequently be tested against empirical evidence. (Source: [[self-wiki/raw/god.md]])
*   **Faith and Inquiry:** While some concepts from a higher belief system may resonate, others require continued questioning and personal application to life experiences. (Source: [[self-wiki/raw/god.md]])
