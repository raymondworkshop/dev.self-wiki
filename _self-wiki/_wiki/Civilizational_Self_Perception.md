> This note contrasts the foundational values and blind spots of major civilizations (Chinese, Western, Indian) regarding concepts of "Self-Awareness," "Good," "Truth," and "Beauty." It suggests that each civilization's collective shadow accommodates its opposite extreme.

## Evolution
- Initial conceptualization of cultural biases regarding morality, truth, and aesthetics.

## Content
### Civilizational Archetypes and Shadow Projection

**Chinese Civilization:**
- **Self-Awareness Benchmark:** "Good" (善).
- **Collective Shadow:** Accommodates "Evil" (恶) over two millennia.
- **Blind Spot:** Tendency to be less sensitive regarding issues of truth versus falsehood; high tolerance for lies.

**Western Civilization:**
- **Self-Awareness Benchmark:** "Truth" (真).
- **Collective Shadow:** Accommodates "Falsehood" (假) over two millennia.
- **Blind Spot:** High intolerance regarding issues of truth, leading to a greater tolerance for immorality (lies, deceit) in pursuit of perceived truth.
- **Value Focus:** Places high value on *harm* and *fairness*, making actions that cause harm or violate fairness the primary moral concern for many Western perspectives.

**Indian Civilization:**
- **Self-Awareness Benchmark:** "Beauty" (美).
- **Collective Shadow:** Projects against various forms of "Ugliness" (不美).

### Operational Directives
- Direct application of theory into action is necessary, rather than relying solely on the theoretical framework: "以经验为基础, 以逻辑为支撑, 互相对照, 互相发展" (Building upon experience, supported by logic, mutually contrasting, and mutually developing).
- The need to "Feel more and open oneself up" (感受多啲，打開自己) is paramount.
