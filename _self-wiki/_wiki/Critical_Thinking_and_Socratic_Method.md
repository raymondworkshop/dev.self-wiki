> This document synthesizes frameworks for rigorous thought, contrasting the scientific method's reliance on empirical evidence with the pitfalls of denialism and dogmatism. It applies these concepts to personal decision-making, communication, and the pursuit of truth.

## Evolution
- Initial thoughts focused on the mechanics of scientific inquiry (Bayesian updating).
- Later application broadened to include interpersonal dynamics, business negotiation, and the dangers of cognitive biases in both self and others.

## Content

### Cognitive Frameworks for Truth Seeking

**1. The Scientific/Socratic Approach (Cyclic Evaluation):**
The pursuit of knowledge must be a dynamic, self-correcting cycle.
*   **Principle:** Knowledge acquisition is a **cyclic process of evaluating** and **changing your mind based on new, better evidence and data**. (Source: [[self-wiki/raw/diary/diary/2022-08-08-the-log-of-your-life.md]])
*   **Bayesian Principle:** Learning is modeled by starting with a **prior guess** (subjective judgment), then using observed data to **update** that guess into a more probable **posterior belief**. (Source: [[self-wiki/raw/diary/diary/2022-08-08-the-log-of-your-life.md]])

**2. Distinguishing Healthy Skepticism from Denialism:**
*   **Skepticism:** Actively welcomes and requires empirical evidence and available data to test hypotheses. (Source: [[self-wiki/raw/diary/diary/2022-08-08-the-log-of-your-life.md]])
*   **Denialism:** The rejection of all empirical evidence, often linked to absolutist views that resist necessary intellectual revision. (Source: [[self-wiki/raw/diary/diary/2022-08-08-the-log-of-your-life.md]])

### Application to Decision Making and Relationships

**1. Decision Making (The Process):**
Effective decisions require moving from high-level conceptual understanding to simple, executable action.
*   **Conceptualization:** Identify the **true problem** and develop a guiding principle for its resolution. (Source: [[self-wiki/raw/diary/diary/2023-01-04.md]])
*   **Execution:** The action plan must be clear regarding *who*, *what*, and *how*. (Source: [[self-wiki/raw/diary/diary/2023-01-04.md]])
*   **Feedback Loop:** Decisions must be continuously tested against actual events. (Source: [[self-wiki/raw/diary/diary/2023-01-04.md]])

**2. Interpersonal Dynamics & Communication:**
*   **Intent vs. Persuasion:** Be mindful of whether the goal is genuine *persuasion* or manipulative *intention*. (Source: [[self-wiki/raw/diary/diary/2022-08-08-the-log-of-your-life.md]])
*   **The Danger of Groupthink:** The tendency of groups to accept flawed premises due to shared biases (e.g., "下愚而上诈" - those who are dull in action but cunning in theory). (Source: [[self-wiki/raw/diary/diary/2022-08-08-the-log-of-your-life.md]])
*   **The Value of Disagreement:** Productive conflict is not personal attack; it is a necessary tool to explore alternatives and ensure all major aspects of a complex issue are vetted. (Source: [[self-wiki/raw/diary/diary/2023-01-04.md]])

### Personal Conduct & Self-Correction
*   **Self-Trust:** One must possess the courage to execute decisions based on one's own sound principles, while remaining flexible enough to pivot when reality proves the initial principle flawed. (Source: [[self-wiki/raw/diary/diary/2023-03-28.md]])
*   **Professional Detachment:** In professional contexts, emotional investment must be managed; the goal is often transactional success, not emotional resonance. (Source: [[self-wiki/raw/diary/diary/2022-08-08-the-log-of-your-life.md]])
