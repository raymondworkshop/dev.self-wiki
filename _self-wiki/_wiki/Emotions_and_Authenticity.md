> This entry integrates reflections on emotional states, the necessary firmness in personal boundaries, and the pursuit of genuine goodness over mere pleasantries. It suggests that true self-possession requires aligning actions with one's core values rather than seeking universal approval.

## Evolution
- Initial thoughts regarding emotional triggers and the pursuit of virtue are being synthesized into a cohesive framework for personal conduct.

## Content
### Emotional States and Alignment
- Feeling more peaceful upon waking later (around 10:00) correlates with reduced self-annoyance.
- A greater sense of happiness is derived from completing pragmatic, real-world tasks (e.g., returning a book) rather than remaining in purely virtual or self-emotional spaces.

### Virtue and Boundaries
- There is a distinction drawn between being "kind" (善良) and being merely "friendly" (友好); the former requires a deeper commitment to integrity.
- It is crucial to maintain one's own standards and not sacrifice personal happiness merely to accommodate others.
- The concept of "身段" (composure/bearing) suggests that while one must have a baseline of conduct, one should not chase after an idealized, perhaps unattainable, standard of conduct ("风骨").
