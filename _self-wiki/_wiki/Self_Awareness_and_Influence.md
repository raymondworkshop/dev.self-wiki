> The most powerful individuals possess the ability to discern the impact others have on them, while simultaneously being able to identify their own internal voice and consciously choose a corresponding reaction. This triad of awareness—external influence, internal monologue, and volitional response—forms the basis of self-mastery.

## Content
### The Triad of Self-Mastery
The capacity to be truly powerful, in a self-reflective sense, rests on three interconnected abilities:
1. **External Discernment**: The ability to accurately perceive and map the influence that others exert upon oneself. (Source: [[self-wiki/raw/origin-apple-notes/ideas  todo.md]])
2. **Internal Discernment**: The capacity to clearly identify and observe one's own internal monologue, biases, and emotional currents without immediate reaction. (Source: [[self-wiki/raw/origin-apple-notes/ideas  todo.md]])
3. **Volitional Response**: The conscious ability to select and enact a response that is congruent with the desired outcome, rather than being driven by automatic reaction patterns. (Source: [[self-wiki/raw/origin-apple-notes/ideas  todo.md]])
