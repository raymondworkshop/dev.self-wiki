> This document synthesizes several streams of thought regarding effective thinking, creative problem-solving, and the integration of rational thought with emotional/value-based judgment. It establishes a framework where creation and improvement must be separated, and deep understanding of fundamentals precedes complex application.

### Distillation (2026-05-20) - source: [[../raw/diary/learning/2022-02-17-notes-on-creative-and-practical-thinking-skills.md]]
### I. Separation of Processes (Creation vs. Improvement)
- **Creation (Building):** Focus on translating mental ideas into the smallest working implementation (MVP). The creator's mind must be unleashed from judgment during initial invention.
- **Improving (Refactoring):** This involves subsequent optimization and algorithmic refinement. One cannot simultaneously invent, edit, and analyze.

### II. Foundations of Clear Thought
- Clear thinkers possess a deep, fundamental understanding of basics in their respective disciplines. Mastery of fundamentals is prioritized over memorizing complex concepts.

### III. Rationality and the Heart (Integrating Mind and Value)
- Rational thought must be trained to work in harmony with deeply held values. Decisions must be vetted against these core values.
- The heart provides intuitive guidance, but it must be balanced with rigorous rational evaluation. This requires seeking all possible data, identifying potential pitfalls, and mapping solutions against highest values.
- **Somatic Connection:** To truly listen to the heart, one must shift attention to the physical feeling in the chest area, grounding the thought process in physical sensation rather than purely mental judgment.

### IV. Creative Thinking Models (De Bono, Moser-Wellman)
- **De Bono's Six Hats:** Provides a structured method for approaching problems by assigning specific roles (White Hat: Facts; Red Hat: Emotion; Black Hat: Caution; Yellow Hat: Benefits; Green Hat: Creativity; Blue Hat: Process) to maintain cognitive separation.
- **Provocative Operation:** Escaping common sense by exaggerating, reversing, or distorting sequences to generate 'crazy ideas,' which are then modified into effective solutions.

### V. Philosophical Underpinnings
- **Aristotle (Eudaimonia):** Ultimate happiness stems from achieving functional excellence by cultivating one's potentials and virtues, guided by the Golden Mean (balance between extremes).
- **Kantian Ethics:** Moral action requires acting only on a maxim that could be universalized (Categorical Imperative), demanding unconditional commitment to duty.
- **Empiricism vs. Positivism:** Science is a cyclic process of hypothesis testing and revision based on new evidence (falsifiability). Theory must be grounded in empirical observation, but observation must be detailed enough to build theory.

**【思维方式的维度】**
* **本质思维 (First Principles)**：在做具体选择时，需回归到最本质的问题。
* **系统思维 (Systems Thinking)**：在理解事物时，需全面看待问题，理解事物运行的整体结构。

**【知识构建与学习】**
* **构建知识体系**：起点是对复杂问题的核心把握；框架是结构化；提炼是个人化。
* **成为专家**：需学习前人的经验，并通过输出进行总结和迁移。
* **学会学习**：应是解决问题的学习、好奇心的探索，以及具备“知道自己不知道”的能力。

**【深度关系与自我】**
* **深度关系的核心**：好的关系建立在完整的内在认知之上，双方都需要对自我保持觉察。
* **构建深度关系**：需双方对自我开放，接纳，并以信任为基石，关注彼此细微处的需要。
* **维持关系**：关键在于理解关系中的角色状态，并能将外在表现（肢体语言、行为）映射到内在信念（Belief）和事实（Fact）层面，进行自我觉察。

### Distillation (2026-05-20) - source: [[../raw/diary/notes/2020-04-29-notes-on-software-engineering.md]]
**Software Engineering = Process + Method + Tool.**

**Project Thinking (The Lifecycle):**
1. **Idea (Problem Identification):** Clearly define the problem and research feasibility.
2. **Concept (Solution Proposal):** Propose conceptual solutions; determine viability and plan execution (scope, resources, time).
3. **Design (Refinement):** Detail the architecture and functional modules based on the concept.
4. **Development (Build & Test):** Implement and iterate based on the design.
5. **Release (Launch):** Present the final result.

**Development Models:**
*   **Waterfall:** Sequential, controlled (Requirements $\rightarrow$ Design $\rightarrow$ Code $\rightarrow$ Test).
*   **Iterative/Agile:** Embraces incremental delivery, constant feedback, and technical debt management (e.g., Scrum + Extreme Programming).

**Product Mindset (MVP Focus):**
*   Focus on the vital few (80/20 rule) and ignore the trivial many.
*   Define a **Minimal Viable Product (MVP)** by focusing on one hypothesis at a time.
*   Spend disproportionate time defining *what* to build versus *how* to build it.

**Soft Skills & Estimation:**
*   **Tell the hard from the impossible:** Make requirements clear; a well-defined problem becomes merely 'hard,' not 'impossible.'
*   **Estimation:** Decompose tasks into small units, restate assumptions, and gain team consensus before committing to a timeline.

### Self-Observation Protocol

**1. Self-Observation (The Data Collection Phase)**
*   **Goal**: To systematically identify important behaviors, recording their frequency and duration. This is a valuable tool for achieving personal growth and making meaningful behavioral adjustments.
*   **Data Points to Capture**: Beyond frequency/duration, record the specific conditions present during the behavior, including the temporal context (time/who involved).
*   **Process Note**: The observation method must remain simple enough to prevent discouragement and ensure continuity.

**2. Structured Reflection (The Analysis Phase)**
*   This phase involves a structured review of recent past events and decisions, coupled with mental rehearsal for upcoming scenarios. It functions as a living record where the date, involved parties/projects, the decision made, and the eventual outcome are logged for comparative analysis.
