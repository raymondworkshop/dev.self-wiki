> The true measure of code or any engineered system is not its initial correctness at a specific point in time, but its ability to maintain correctness through low-cost evolution over time. A highly complex system, such as a 100,000-line function, inherently resists this necessary evolution.

## Evolution
- Initial concepts regarding system robustness are being formalized into a principle contrasting initial functionality versus evolutionary maintainability.

## Content
### Code Evolution Principle
The importance of code lies not in whether it runs correctly at a specific moment, but in its capacity to maintain correctness through low-cost evolution over time.
> (Source: [[self-wiki/raw/origin-apple-notes/engineering.md]])

### Complexity Constraint
A complex function comprising 100,000 lines of code is inherently difficult to evolve sustainably.
> (Source: [[self-wiki/raw/origin-apple-notes/engineering.md]])
