> This document synthesizes the philosophical view that mathematics provides the rigorous, absolute logical framework necessary for true science. It contrasts the absolute, binary logic derived from certain religious contracts with the probabilistic, subjective logic often found in human discourse.

## Evolution
- Initial concepts regarding the relationship between religion, logic, and mathematics are being formalized into a cohesive model. The contrast between absolute logical systems (like those implied by certain theological frameworks) and empirical observation is being mapped out.

## Content
### The Foundation of Science in Logic
The rigorous development of a consistent logical system, combined with its application, is what allows mathematics to serve as the leading edge of science. This logical structure must be sound and absolute.

### The Role of Absolute Logic (Theological Parallel)
The text draws a parallel with ancient Israelite religion, where an absolute covenant with a singular God necessitated a pursuit of absolute logical consistency to maintain the covenant. This high-stakes, absolute requirement drives the development of formal logic.
*   **Binary Truth:** The covenant is absolute; it is either upheld or broken, leaving no room for ambiguity. This necessity breeds the **Law of Contradiction** (a statement cannot be both true and false) and the **Law of Excluded Middle** (a statement must be either true or false).
*   **Formal Logic:** These two principles, when applied to a supreme, absolute entity (like the God in the analogy), generate the bedrock of formal logic, which then becomes the source of mathematics.

### Contrast with Practical/Human Discourse (Ancient China)
In contrast, ancient Chinese discourse, while valuing logical debate, often lacked the drive toward a perfectly consistent, formal system. Debates were often contingent on the relationship and ability of the speakers to persuade each other, leading to a focus on subjective understanding rather than objective, absolute truth. This reliance on "persuasion technique" rather than objective structure prevented the elevation of local logic to universal formal logic.

### Induction vs. Deduction
*   **Induction:** Human experience is often inductive, moving from specific observations (particular statements) to general conclusions (universal statements). However, induction is inherently probabilistic; it can only suggest a "possibly correct" law, never guarantee it.
*   **Deduction:** Mathematics, when based on absolute axioms (like the binary laws derived from the analogy), allows for deductive reasoning to reach necessary conclusions.

### The Power of Formal Logic
The absolute nature of formal logic, underpinned by the Law of Contradiction and the Law of Excluded Middle, provides the necessary "momentum" for mathematics to achieve its immense productive power, allowing for methods like *reductio ad absurdum* (proof by contradiction).

### The Limits of Thought
The concept of "emptiness" ($\text{空}$) challenges the very basis of formal logic. While formal logic operates within its defined boundaries, philosophical traditions like Buddhism suggest a reality beyond binary existence, where concepts like "existence" and "non-existence" are not mutually exclusive in the way formal logic demands.

### Modern Application: Capitalism and Ownership
The rise of modern capitalism is linked to the concept of "private ownership," which requires the owner to possess absolute and comprehensive dominion over their property. This absolute ownership, rooted in a metaphysical concept of ultimate ownership (analogous to the Creator's ownership in Christianity), allows for the abstract application of mathematical laws (like the Law of Identity, Contradiction, and Excluded Middle) in market transactions.

## Evolution
- The initial notes are moving from a purely anecdotal comparison to a structured philosophical model. The integration of the three laws of classical logic (Contradiction, Excluded Middle, Non-Contradiction) as the necessary condition for mathematical rigor is becoming clearer.
