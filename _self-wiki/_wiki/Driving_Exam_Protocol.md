> This document synthesizes detailed operational protocols for driving exams, covering everything from vehicle startup checks to complex maneuvers like lane changes and intersections. Adherence to these protocols emphasizes defensive driving, situational awareness, and respecting all road users.

## Evolution
- Initial notes were fragmented procedural checklists. They have been synthesized into a structured protocol covering startup, driving modes, and specific maneuvers to ensure comprehensive coverage for the exam context.

## Content

### 1. Vehicle Startup and Shutdown Protocol
*   **Pre-Drive Check (Co-pilot/Instructor):** Check mirrors (side and center) upon entry.
*   **Starting:** Check left, center, and right mirrors for safety; release handbrake, start engine.
*   **Parking/Stopping:** Check mirrors; apply handbrake, shift to Park (P), turn off ignition, release seatbelt.
*   **General Rule:** All starting and stopping actions require checking the center and side mirrors.

### 2. Road Situational Awareness & Driving Modes
*   **Maintaining Distance:** Keep a safe distance equivalent to about two car lengths when approaching/passing objects.
*   **High-Speed/Specific Routes (e.g., 牛皮沙线):**
    *   Must wait 4 seconds without oncoming traffic before proceeding.
    *   Entering/Exiting: If there is a parked car on the left, signal, check mirrors, check blind spot, proceed slowly. If oncoming traffic emerges on the right, apply brakes immediately and wait for safety clearance before proceeding.
*   **Riverbank Line (河畔线):**
    *   Overtaking stationary vehicles: If oncoming traffic is present, proceed slowly. If no oncoming traffic, proceed. If oncoming traffic is present and the overtaken vehicle is stationary, find a safe spot to pull over.
    *   Monitor buses on the roadside and pull over when necessary.
*   **Silver City Line (银城线):**
    *   Initial movement: Turn left, drive on the left side (e.g., 牛皮沙街 $\rightarrow$ 插桅杆街).
    *   Lane Change: On 小沥源路 $\rightarrow$ 插桅杆街, turn left, drive on the right side.
    *   **Intersection Rule:** Avoid parallel turns at intersections; the outer lane should turn slightly later, and the inner lane slightly earlier.
    *   **Right Turn:** Must yield to straight-through traffic.

### 3. Maneuvering Techniques
*   **Emergency Braking:** Apply brakes halfway and judge the situation based on the signal light (黄灯).
*   **Right Turn:** Accelerate forward, begin turning when reaching the safety island, maintaining awareness of the entrance and lane lines to avoid obstructing the median.
*   **Left Turn:** Begin turning after crossing the left white line, ensuring the turn encompasses the bend without obstructing the median.
*   **Parallel Turns:** When two vehicles turn in parallel, the outer vehicle should enter the intersection before turning the wheel to maintain safe distance from the inner vehicle.
*   **Overtaking Stationary Vehicles:** Observe and signal after 1.5–2 car lengths behind the stationary vehicle. Hesitation or blocking traffic without reason is penalized; aggressive overtaking in blind corners or with oncoming traffic is dangerous.

### 4. Core Driving Philosophy (Crucial for Scoring)
*   **Yielding to Other Road Users:** Always maintain situational awareness and act appropriately. If other vehicles yield, you may proceed. However, if any situation arises, you have the option to wait in place rather than forcing movement or expecting others to yield. Forcing movement or expecting others to accommodate is a critical error ("不顧其他道路使用者").
*   **Proximity to Objects:** When overtaking obstacles, maintain at least the width of one car door's clearance. If clearance is minimal, proceed slowly or prepare to stop.
