> This raw data presents a rich, multi-lingual compendium of principles regarding negotiation, professional conduct, and interpersonal dynamics. The core theme revolves around shifting from positional bargaining (Rights/Power) to interest-based problem-solving, while also detailing best practices for professional communication across different cultural contexts.



### Distillation (2026-05-20) - source: [[../raw/diary/softskills/2016-04-03-improve-your-softskills.md]]
**Negotiation Strategy (Interest vs. Rights/Power):**
- Successful negotiation requires understanding the underlying needs and interests of the other party, rather than rigidly adhering to stated positions (Rights/Power).
- To shift the focus, employ 'why' and 'why not' questions and attempt to generate multiple options, rewarding the use of interest-based approaches.
- Rights/Power tactics are only warranted when the other party refuses to negotiate in good faith, or when all interest-based options have been exhausted.

**Professional Communication Protocols:**
- **Good News:** Should ideally be delivered face-to-face or via phone to convey sincerity and weight.
- **Bad News:** Can often be managed via email, provided it is not highly sensitive or legally binding.
- **Subjective Matters:** Should be communicated face-to-face to minimize the risk of leaving lingering textual evidence that could be misinterpreted.

**Career & Team Dynamics:**
- **Ownership:** Individuals must take ownership of their actions and contributions.
- **Feedback:** Proactively seeking feedback and being gracious in receiving it is crucial for growth.
- **Reputation Management:** Maintaining a proactive, unbiased reputation is vital; one must manage how they are perceived by others.
