> This framework outlines a structured approach to engaging with fictional works, treating them not just as entertainment but as mirrors for life experience. The process moves sequentially from detailed observation to abstract internalization and finally to practical application in one's own life.

## Evolution
- Initial thoughts were unstructured notes on a reading experience. The framework has since been formalized into a multi-stage cognitive process to ensure maximum extraction of meaning from narrative input.

## Content
### The Reading Synthesis Protocol
When engaging with a text, the following stages must be executed to achieve deep cognitive engagement:
1. **Describe the Event**: Accurately map out the narrative occurrences.
2. **Sketch the Details**: Capture the nuances, setting, and character motivations.
3. **Find the Allegory/Meaning**: Determine the underlying theme or universal truth the fiction represents.
4. **Connect to Life**: Bridge the gap between the fictional scenario and real-world experience.
5. **Articulate Reflection**: Formulate personal insights derived from the synthesis.

### Case Study: *Red and White Roses* & Martin Eden
The reading of *Red and White Roses* and the parallel study of Martin Eden serve as case studies illustrating the necessity of this rigorous application. The initial barrier encountered by the reader ("can't read because of too much self imagination") highlights the danger of intellectualizing without grounding the theory in the text's reality.
