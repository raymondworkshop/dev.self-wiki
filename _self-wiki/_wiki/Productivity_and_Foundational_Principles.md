> True productivity requires aligning physical and mental states; low-quality time arises from neglecting basic needs like sleep, nutrition, and exercise. Furthermore, complex problems must be decomposed, mirroring the need to clearly define the root problem before attempting solutions.

### Distillation (2026-05-20) - source: [[../raw/diary/softskills/2015-07-29-productivity.md]]
**Productivity Framework (2015-07-29 Distillation)**

**1. Physical/Mental Baseline Maintenance:**
*   Acknowledge the body's state: Hunger, fatigue, or restlessness equate to low-quality cognitive time.
*   Prescription: Proactive management of diet, sleep, and exercise is non-negotiable for high-quality output.

**2. Problem Decomposition:**
*   For 'hard problems,' the initial step must be systematic breakdown.

**3. Design Thinking Integration:**
*   The process must begin with accurately defining the *own* needs and the *true* problem being addressed, rather than jumping to solutions. (Corresponds to: 搞清自身需求 $\rightarrow$ 定义真正的问题)
