> Consciousness, when intentionally ordered, acts as a mirror reflecting internal and external information, allowing for a self-directed reality. The ability to sustain focused attention against internal and external entropy is key to achieving "flow," a state where life experiences become deeply meaningful and enjoyable. This disciplined investment of attention is the primary mechanism through which the self develops complexity and grows.

## Evolution
- Initial concepts regarding consciousness ordering and attention investment have been refined into a model explaining the mechanics of "flow" and its relationship to self-growth.

## Content
### Consciousness as Ordered Information
Consciousness functions as a mirror, selectively reflecting information about both internal nervous system activity and external reality. It possesses the capacity to override genetic programming and chart its own course. The ability to persevere through obstacles is a quality often admired, and developing this requires mastering the ordering of consciousness to maintain control over thoughts and feelings.

**Intentions:** Consciousness can be ordered toward specific goals or intentions. This intention is the driving force that maintains information in its ordered state, even if the *why* behind the intention is not consciously articulated.

### The Role of Attention
Most individuals operate below peak information processing capacity. Therefore, the information allowed into consciousness dictates the quality of life. A person in control of consciousness exhibits the ability to:
1. Focus attention intentionally.
2. Remain oblivious to distractions.
3. Concentrate for the necessary duration without lingering unnecessarily.

We shape our reality through how we invest our limited attention. If attention is diffused randomly, the experience is scattered; if it is focused like a beam of energy, a coherent reality emerges.

### Achieving Flow State
The "flow experience" occurs when attention is freely invested toward a chosen goal without internal disorder or external threat. This state is characterized by:
*   **Deep Engagement:** The activity itself becomes enjoyable and purposeful.
*   **Psychic Energy Investment:** The individual successfully invests psychic energy into self-chosen goals.
*   **Self-Control:** The individual maintains command over their psychic energy, adding order to consciousness through action.

**Psychic Entropy:** The primary threat to consciousness is psychic disorder—information conflicting with existing intentions or distracting from them. Winning the battle against this entropy is a struggle for self-control over attention.

### Self-Growth through Complexity
The self is central to consciousness. Attention shapes the self, and the self, in turn, shapes attention. Following a flow episode, the self undergoes increased complexity through two processes:
1.  **Differentiation:** Moving toward uniqueness by successfully overcoming challenges, leading to greater capability and skill.
2.  **Integration:** Bringing autonomous parts of the self into a cohesive whole, facilitated by the high degree of order achieved during deep concentration.

When we act freely for the sake of the action itself, we transcend our previous state. This cycle—choosing a goal, investing concentration, achieving flow, and experiencing complexity—is the mechanism of self-growth.
