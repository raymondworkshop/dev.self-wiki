> This page synthesizes core principles for self-preservation, emphasizing the necessity of setting firm boundaries in both personal and professional spheres. It addresses the wisdom of knowing when and how to disengage from draining situations or individuals.

## Evolution
- Initial insights regarding the need for self-protection and the practical application of saying "no" to maintain equilibrium.

## Content
### Self Care Principles
*   **学会拒绝 (Learn to Refuse):** The proactive skill of saying no is crucial for protecting one's time, energy, and mental space.
*   **不说太满的话 (Don't Say Too Much):** Prudence in speech is vital; restraint prevents over-commitment and misunderstanding.
*   **遇到烂人烂事，及早抽身 (When encountering bad people or bad situations, withdraw early):** Recognizing toxicity and executing timely disengagement is a form of high-level self-management.
*   **做事说话有plan B (Have a Plan B for actions and words):** Contingency planning mitigates the fallout from unforeseen negative outcomes.
*   **对他人保留三分 (Keep three parts reserved for others):** Maintaining emotional and situational distance prevents burnout and protects against undue imposition.
