> This principle outlines that the search for meaning is inherently personal and that life's purpose must be actively answered by the individual. Meaning can be discovered through creating value, experiencing profound reality, or bravely confronting unavoidable suffering.

## Evolution
- Initial concepts regarding personal responsibility in defining life's meaning are being integrated with the structured framework of Logotherapy.

## Content
### The Burden and Privilege of Meaning
The search for meaning is the primary driver of existence. This meaning is not external to be found but is uniquely and specifically the individual's responsibility to fulfill. Life does not provide the answer; it poses the question, and the individual must answer for their own life. This acceptance of responsibility is the core of human existence according to Logotherapy.

### Three Paths to Discovering Meaning
Logotherapy posits three avenues through which meaning can be discovered:

1. **Creation/Deed (Achievement):** Finding meaning through tangible works or accomplishments.
2. **Experience/Encounter:** Finding meaning by experiencing profound realities (beauty, truth, goodness) or through deep connection with another unique human being (love). Love, in this context, allows one to see and actualize potential in both partners.
3. **Attitude toward Suffering:** Finding meaning by bravely accepting and confronting unavoidable suffering.

### The Activism of Existence
Life's potentialities are transient; they become reality only when actualized. Logotherapy views existence not as passive endurance but as an active process. Like a scholar meticulously documenting a journey, the individual who actively engages with life's challenges documents each phase, allowing for future reflection with pride and joy on a life lived to its fullest.
