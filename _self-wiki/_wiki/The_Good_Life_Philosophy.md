> This page synthesizes thoughts on what constitutes a "Good Life," balancing spiritual grounding with practical mental and emotional fitness. It suggests that a noble purpose, combined with mental clarity and emotional kindness, forms a sustainable foundation for well-being.

## Evolution
- Initial thoughts were fragmented, drawing from various philosophical sources. This entry begins to structure these ideas into actionable components: spiritual alignment, mental fitness, and noble purpose.

## Content
### Components of a Fulfilling Life

**1. Spiritual Foundation:**
- The concept of a "Good Life" is viewed through a spiritual lens, specifically referencing Christianity in its literal form, while maintaining a personal spiritual rather than strictly religious orientation. (Source: [[self-wiki/raw/diary/learning/2021-02-20-update-on-the-good-life.md]])

**2. Habit Formation (Inspired by Atomic Habits):**
- Sustainable habits must be driven by noble, overarching purposes. (Source: [[self-wiki/raw/diary/learning/2021-02-20-update-on-the-good-life.md]])

**3. Mental and Emotional Fitness:**
- Achieving mental/emotional fitness is crucial and involves three pillars:
    - **Emotional State:** Being relaxed, happy, and kind.
    - **Cognitive State:** Being clear-minded, calm, and intelligent. (Source: [[self-wiki/raw/diary/learning/2021-02-20-update-on-the-good-life.md]])

### References and Context
- This framework draws upon various philosophical texts, including Bertrand Russell's views on happiness and love, and foundational texts on critical thinking and philosophy. (Source: [[self-wiki/raw/diary/learning/2021-02-20-update-on-the-good-life.md]])
