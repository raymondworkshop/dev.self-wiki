> A good mentor's role is to guide a learner to achieve their first success, providing the necessary initial experience to build confidence and a sense of accomplishment. True mastery in any field requires successfully executing a concept at least once to move beyond theory and into practical application.

## Evolution
- Initial thoughts on learning suggest that sheer volume of study is insufficient; practical application leading to a "feeling of success" is the critical gateway to deeper learning.

## Content
### The Role of the Mentor (启蒙老师)
A good mentor acts as a lighthouse, helping the learner achieve their first successful outcome. Once this initial success is achieved, the learner gains both the "feeling of success" and the "feeling of learning," enabling them to self-correct and synthesize future experiences.

### The Necessity of First Success
In any domain—be it innovative projects, learning to ride a bicycle, discerning people, or investing—theoretical knowledge alone is insufficient. One must achieve a successful execution once to truly enter the domain. Without this initial success, accumulating theory daily yields no practical entry point.
