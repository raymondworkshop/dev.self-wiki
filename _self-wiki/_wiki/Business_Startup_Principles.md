> This document synthesizes foundational principles for entrepreneurial endeavors, emphasizing the necessity of deep focus, market fit, and continuous learning through interaction. Success requires aligning personal passion with market needs while maintaining adaptability.

## Evolution
- Initial concepts regarding business ventures were fragmented across various notes. This synthesis attempts to structure these ideas into actionable principles for modern startups.

## Content
### Core Philosophy
*   **Purpose Driven:** The venture must have a clear "why" (Purpose) that drives the business plan. The ultimate goal is often linked to a larger life meaning, such as "Help" or "Self-Actualization."
*   **Product/Market Fit is Paramount:** The product must genuinely solve a problem. The key question is: "Does the product solve a problem?"
*   **Focus and Depth:** Innovation requires intense focus ("专注到一点上") to achieve scale. Superficial attempts lead to stagnation.
*   **The Role of Presence:** The founder must be deeply involved, not just conceptualizing. "不能只想， 要去做" (Don't just think, you must act).

### Execution & Growth Vectors
*   **Business Models:** Ideas range from software development (AI, SaaS, language learning apps) to service provision (Online Travel/Homestay Agency).
*   **Scaling & Acquisition:** For larger ventures, the strategy might involve acquiring smaller companies whose CEOs possess proven execution capabilities ("买一些小公司 ceo").
*   **Market Context (Domestic vs. International):** Domestic markets often have different expectations regarding the scope of work required from the founder compared to international markets.
*   **The Founder's Role:** The founder must be capable of leading from both the technical/product side ("Product leadership - from bottom up") and the vision/culture side ("Sales leadership").

### Personal & Team Dynamics
*   **The Founder's Self-Management:** Before leading a company, one must lead oneself. "首先要经营好你自己， 活出自己."
*   **Team Synergy:** Founding teams and investors must operate on a basis of equality, mutual support, and synergy. Decisions involving veto power require extreme caution.
*   **The Value of Dialogue:** "多见人，多和人聊天" is crucial. Conversations with diverse people and stories are vital for challenging ingrained biases and shifting cognition.
*   **The Role of Money:** Money is a tool for convenience and enabling experiences ("钱是用来便利的").

### Key Success Factors
*   **The Offering:** What you do must be "Interesting, different from others, profitable, and useful to others," with no shortcuts.
*   **Mindset:** Maintain a "Can Do" attitude, backed by cognitive maturity, experience, and capital.
*   **Humility in Teaching:** If teaching, the role is collaborative exploration, not omniscience.
