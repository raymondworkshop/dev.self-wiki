### Self-Confidence and Core Assets
Self-confidence is not innate but is an outcome of successfully understanding, valuing, and applying one's core assets. This leads to greater happiness and resilience.

**Finding & Nurturing Strengths:**
*   **Personality Features:** Friendliness, open-mindedness, reliability.
*   **Character Traits:** Kindness, grit, patience, guilelessness/honesty, stubbornness (倔强).
*   **Talents & Gifts:** Enjoying beauty, logical thinking, sociology (TOCHECK: Identify activities where one finds things easy).

**Areas for Improvement (To Improve):**
*   **Power & Money:** Must be accompanied by being better than others.
*   **Relational Expression:** The ability to make those you care about feel genuinely valued (requires expression).
*   **Business Acumen:** Prioritizing self-interest.
*   **Practical Experience:** Focusing on *how* things are done, not just *why*.
*   **Filtering:** Knowing how to select one's peers/friends.

### Empathy: Connecting Through "We"
Empathy is crucial for building relationships and bringing out the best in humanity.

**Affective Empathy (Feeling With):**
*   Recognizing and feeling the emotions of others.
*   **Reading Nonverbal Cues:** Interpreting facial expressions, gestures, posture, and tone (happy, sad, stressed, scared, frustrated, angry).
*   **Active Inquiry:** Asking questions like, "How did that make you feel?" or "Are you anxious, worried, sad?"
*   **Safe Sharing:** Expressing one's own feelings vulnerably (e.g., "I'm frustrated with this book.").
*   **Deep Listening:** Maintaining eye contact or focusing on the speaker's context, and attempting to find one common ground ("we both like...").
*   **Labeling Emotions:** Articulating observed feelings ("You sound stressed," "You seem angry").

**Cognitive Empathy (Perspective Taking):**
*   Stepping into another's shoes to understand their viewpoint, thoughts, and desires.

**Behavioral Empathy (Acting with Compassion):**
*   Taking action to help: Consoling ("I'm sorry"), Assisting ("Do u need help?"), Reassuring ("I'm here for you"), or simply empathizing ("I know how you feel").

### Self-Control and Integrity
Self-control involves managing one's focus, impulses, and thoughts.

**Focus on What Matters:**
*   Mindfulness of internal/external stimuli: body sensations, sounds, present reality.
*   Self-Awareness Check: Identifying feelings and tracing the cause ("What's causing it?").
*   De-escalation: Using deep breathing to calm down.
*   Positive Self-Talk: Affirmations like, "I think I can," or "I can handle this."
*   **Action Sequence:** Stop, Think, Act Right.

**Integrity:**
*   Possessing a strong moral compass: Knowing what is right, caring about what is right, and doing what is right.
*   This establishes boundaries, resists temptation, and provides peace of mind, leading to authenticity.
