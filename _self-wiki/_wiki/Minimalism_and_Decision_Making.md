> This page synthesizes principles advocating for intentional reduction in planning and activity volume, alongside observations on the cognitive bias of satisficing. It emphasizes the need to pause even during peak enjoyment and to avoid choice overload.

## Evolution
- Initial concepts regarding the quality vs. quantity of experiences are being integrated with the cognitive bias of "satisficing" (choosing the first acceptable option).

## Content
### The Practice of Less (Minimalism in Action)
- **Activity Planning**: Avoid over-planning; focus on doing fewer things, but doing them well.
- **Experience Quality**: When enjoying something, pause periodically to rest and reset.
- **Intentional Difficulty**: Occasionally engage in activities that feel slightly uncomfortable to foster growth.

### Satisficing Decision Making
- **Choice Overload**: Recognize and avoid the trap of excessive options.
- **Satisficing Bias**: Understand that the tendency to accept the first option that meets minimum criteria is a cognitive shortcut, not always the optimal path.
