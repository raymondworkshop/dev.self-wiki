> This document synthesizes multiple perspectives on the process of learning, moving from foundational principles to advanced mastery techniques. It emphasizes the cyclical nature of learning through theory, practice, feedback, and reflection.

## Evolution
- Initial thoughts were fragmented across various sources, touching upon first principles, practical application, and the psychological aspects of mastery. This synthesis structures these ideas into a cohesive framework.

## Content

### Foundational Learning Principles
*   **First Principles Approach**: Begin by establishing a deep, first-principles understanding of a subject before exploring specialized or interesting areas. (Source: [[self-wiki/raw/diary/learning/2020-05-13-about-how-to-learn.md]])
*   **Iterative Depth**: After establishing a basic foundation, engage in practical application. Discovering problems during practice then necessitates targeted research and learning to solve those problems. (Source: [[self-wiki/raw/diary/learning/2020-05-13-about-how-to-learn.md]])
*   **Confidence Building**: Confidence in a new skill can be built in three ways: observing experts, achieving a first-principles understanding, or developing confidence in one's capacity to learn anything. (Source: [[self-wiki/raw/diary/learning/2020-05-13-about-how-to-learn.md]])

### Mastery and Deep Practice (The Journey)
*   **The Plateau of Practice**: Mastery is found in the present moment. It requires maintaining full awareness of one's mental, physical, and emotional state during the activity. The routine itself can become deeply rewarding and integral to one's identity. (Source: [[self-wiki/raw/diary/learning/2020-05-13-about-how-to-learn.md]])
*   **Feedback Loop (Practice $\rightarrow$ Feedback $\rightarrow$ Practice)**: The most effective learning cycle involves applying theory to practice, seeking critical feedback, and then applying the newly gained knowledge in the next cycle. (Source: [[self-wiki/raw/diary/learning/2020-05-13-about-how-to-learn.md]])
*   **Mental Visualization**: To achieve high performance, one must hold the desired outcome mentally, visualizing the execution vividly, as if the action were already occurring. (Source: [[self-wiki/raw/diary/learning/2020-05-13-about-how-to-learn.md]])

### Cognitive and Interpersonal Strategies
*   **Seeking Guidance**: When learning from a master, humility, patience, and empathy are crucial. The interaction itself must be valued, maintaining a proper psychological distance to remain a student, not just a disciple. (Source: [[self-wiki/raw/diary/learning/2020-05-13-about-how-to-learn.md]])
*   **Truth and Honesty**: Truth-telling is most effective when it involves revealing one's own feelings, rather than being used to insult others or manipulate outcomes. (Source: [[self-wiki/raw/diary/learning/2020-05-13-about-how-to-learn.md]])
*   **Prioritization**: Indecision drains energy. It is better to make a wrong choice than no choice at all, provided one has taken time for wise planning and committed to moving forward. (Source: [[self-wiki/raw/diary/learning/2020-05-13-about-how-to-learn.md]])

### Application of Ideas
*   **Learning by Doing/Osmosis**: Active engagement, such as explaining ideas or discussing them with others, accelerates learning far beyond passive intake. (Source: [[self-wiki/raw/diary/learning/2020-05-13-about-how-to-learn.md]])
*   **Rest and Consolidation**: The importance of sleep cannot be overstated in the consolidation of learning into long-term memory. (Source: [[self-wiki/raw/diary/learning/2020-05-13-about-how-to-learn.md]])
