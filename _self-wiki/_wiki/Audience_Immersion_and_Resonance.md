> The inability to connect with fictional characters mirrors a failure to deeply engage with the material, suggesting that true appreciation requires mutual emotional investment. If the viewer remains detached, the experience remains intellectually observed rather than viscerally felt, rendering judgment subjective.

## Evolution
- Initial thoughts focused on the mechanics of critical viewing versus emotional investment. The current synthesis frames this as a necessary condition for personal resonance.

## Content
The core observation is that for a viewer to find a narrative valuable or "good," there must be a reciprocal journey. If the audience member cannot emotionally invest—sharing in the characters' joys and sorrows—the viewing experience becomes purely observational, lacking the depth required to feel truly moved or to validate the work's quality. The inability to achieve this shared emotional space prevents the formation of a deep, meaningful connection with the narrative.
