> Intellectual discussions often fail due to a lack of foundational rigor, characterized by conceptual drift and unsupported assertions. True advancement requires grounding arguments in verifiable evidence and maintaining strict adherence to defined terms.

## Evolution
- Initial thoughts focused on the breakdown of academic discourse. This page evolves to codify the *symptoms* (concept shifting, lack of evidence) and the *antidote* (precision, grounding).

## Content
### The Pitfalls of Unstructured Discourse
In academic or deep discussions, the tendency to jump between subjects without establishing a common ground is highly detrimental.
1. **Conceptual Ambiguity (偷换概念)**: Participants fail to maintain a shared, precise definition of terms, allowing the discussion to derail into tangential noise.
2. **Lack of Empirical Basis (立论不讲证据)**: Assertions are made without supporting data, rendering the argument speculative rather than substantive. Even when evidence is mentioned, its lack of quantitative or qualitative backing undermines its validity.

### Principles for Rigorous Exchange
To move beyond unproductive chatter, the following standards must be enforced:
*   **Define Terms First**: Before proceeding, ensure all participants agree on the operational definitions of key concepts.
*   **Evidence Tracing**: Every major claim must be traceable to its supporting data or logical premise.
*   **Structured Argumentation**: Maintain focus on one core thesis until it has been thoroughly defended against challenges.
