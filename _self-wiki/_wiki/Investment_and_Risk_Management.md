> This document synthesizes notes regarding insurance products, investment strategies, and the necessary mindset required when navigating professional and social environments. It contrasts the theoretical aspects of financial planning with the practical realities of human interaction.

## Evolution
- Initial notes were highly granular, focusing on specific insurance product details and costs. The synthesis has begun to frame these details within broader principles of risk management and self-positioning.

## Content

### Insurance Principles
*   **Types of Coverage**: Basic coverage includes Health Insurance, Medical Insurance, and Life Insurance.
*   **Planning**: It is crucial to select appropriate insurance early on, such as critical illness insurance or accident insurance.
*   **Cost Management**: Plans like the "Bowtie Insurance" or specific plans from providers (e.g., AIA, Prudential) offer options like riders or deductibles to manage premiums based on the level of coverage desired.
*   **Specific Products**: Notes mention specific plans like "恒生银行为你提供合资格延期年金保单..." (HSBC annuity plans) and various premium structures.

### Investment & Financial Context
*   **Investment Vehicles**: Mention of specific funds, such as "~500/M @ HSBC Global Equity Index Fund," indicates a focus on indexed fund investing.
*   **Costs**: Transactional costs noted include exam fees (220 HKD) and insurance fees (252 HKD).

### Socio-Professional Context & Self-Positioning
*   **The Reality Check**: A significant observation is that professional settings are inherently "arenas of interest" (利益场). Success requires understanding this dynamic.
*   **Filtering & Authenticity**: The observation that "this world doesn't have many independent thinkers or purely benevolent people" necessitates developing the skill to filter relationships and being cautious about trusting words alone, valuing body language and "vibe" more highly.
*   **Self-Positioning**: The need to move beyond being overly accommodating ("too nice") and to establish a peer-level footing with others, especially when growing up in a close-knit community ("熟人社会").
