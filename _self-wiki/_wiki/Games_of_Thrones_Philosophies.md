> This page analyzes the philosophical undercurrents present in the source material, using the fictional narrative as a mirror for real-world decision-making, power dynamics, and the tension between idealism and pragmatism. It explores the cost of uncompromising vision versus the necessity of counsel and restraint.

## Evolution
- Initial notes were raw reactions to the source material. This page structures those reactions into actionable philosophical models, linking the fictional drama to cognitive frameworks.

## Content
### The Tension Between Vision and Pragmatism
The narrative presents a critical tension between unwavering vision (Daenerys's initial drive) and the pragmatic counsel required to navigate complex realities.
> "A foreign where you can’t abduct, beat, or intimidate" highlights the difference between theoretical power and applied reality. (Source: [[self-wiki/raw/diary/notes/2022-03-13-games-of-thones.md]])

### The Role of Counsel and Restraint
The effectiveness of a leader is shown to depend heavily on the quality of their counsel. Unchecked idealism, even if rooted in good intent, can lead to catastrophic outcomes.
> "She chose an advisor who would check her worst impulses instead of feeding them." This illustrates the vital function of a sounding board capable of challenging the core vision. (Source: [[self-wiki/raw/diary/notes/2022-03-13-games-of-thones.md]])

### Accountability and Consequence
The material forces a confrontation with personal accountability, where actions have irreversible costs, including the cost to the future.
> "I don’t care why you did what you did. I only care what it cost us. It cost us the future." This frames outcomes not just as successes or failures, but as a measurable toll on potential. (Source: [[self-wiki/raw/diary/notes/2022-03-13-games-of-thones.md]])

### Self-Validation vs. External Reality
The journey involves reconciling one's internal truth or conviction with the harsh, often unforgiving, external reality.
> "you are right, you're not guilty of your father's crimes. And I'm not beholden to my ancestor's vows." This speaks to the necessary cognitive break from inherited narratives or expectations. (Source: [[self-wiki/raw/diary/notes/2022-03-13-games-of-thones.md]])
