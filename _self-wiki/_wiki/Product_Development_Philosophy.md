> The most valuable insights for product development arise from tackling difficult technical problems. Successful product building requires starting with the user experience and vision, rather than jumping into technical implementation details.
>
> This philosophy balances ambitious vision (moonshots) with rigorous execution, ensuring the technology serves the user need, not the other way around.

## Evolution
- Initial concepts regarding product management were fragmented across various sources. This synthesis integrates the "Product Manager" notes with structured methodologies from Marty Cagan's framework (Empathize, ASR, etc.) to create a cohesive philosophy.

## Content

### Core Tenets of Product Building

**1. Focus on User Value (The "Why"):**
*   **User-Centricity:** The primary focus must always be on the user. Technology exists to solve a specific customer problem. (Source: [[self-wiki/raw/diary/tech/2020-02-11-notes-on-product-manager.md]])
*   **Vision First:** Begin by building the use case and defining the protocol second. Customers buy the outcome/experience, not the underlying technology. (Source: [[self-wiki/raw/diary/tech/2020-02-11-notes-on-product-manager.md]])
*   **Empathy Driven Discovery:** Deeply understand the users by talking to them and identifying the gaps in their current reality. (Source: [[self-wiki/raw/diary/tech/2020-02-11-notes-on-product-manager.md]])

**2. Strategic Execution (The "How"):**
*   **Ambitious Goal Setting:** Do not shy away from big goals; big goals require big thinking. (Source: [[self-wiki/raw/diary/tech/2020-02-11-notes-on-product-manager.md]])
*   **Defining Success (Business Goals):** Clearly articulate the business goal using a measurable structure: `Subject + Measurable Outcome + Context (The Need)`. (Source: [[self-wiki/raw/diary/tech/2020-02-11-notes-on-product-manager.md]])
*   **Architectural Rigor:** For complex systems, the architecture must be defined early to support the desired quality attributes (e.g., performance, availability). The architecture defines the major building blocks and their communication rules. (Source: [[self-wiki/raw/diary/tech/2020-02-11-notes-on-product-manager.md]])

**3. The Builder's Mindset:**
*   **Proactive Ownership:** Embrace the role of the builder. Seeing one's creation function successfully is a powerful validation of the process. (Source: [[self-wiki/raw/diary/tech/2020-02-11-notes-on-product-manager.md]])
*   **Confidence in Overcoming:** When facing novel challenges inherent in new ventures, maintain confidence that obstacles can be surmounted through disciplined effort. (Source: [[self-wiki/raw/diary/tech/2020-02-11-notes-on-product-manager.md]])

### Product Development Lifecycle Stages (Synthesis)

*   **Discovery (Empathize):** Talk to the right stakeholders, map the network, and define *why* they care (Business Goals).
*   **Definition (ASR):** Define the problem from the user's POV (Problem Definition). Specify requirements clearly, ensuring the user drives the functionality. Capture constraints and quality attributes as measurable scenarios.
*   **Design/Build (Architecture):** Choose structures (patterns) that promote desired quality attributes. Assign specific functional responsibilities to each element.
*   **Decision Making:** Use structured processes (e.g., design studios) to move from exploration to commitment.
