## Organizational Intelligence & Leadership Roles
- **Trusted Advisors/Boundary Spanners**: Identify individuals who span functional units and connect ideas; nurturing these relationships is key to organizational health.
- **Teamwork Model**: Military parallel suggests decision-making authority rests with officers, but execution and expert advice often flow from NCOs, highlighting the critical role of frontline expertise.

## Cultural Intelligence (CQ) Framework
- **Adaptability**: The capability to adjust effectively to new cultural contexts, requiring deep understanding of the other party's worldview.
- **Face Cultures**: Self-worth is derived from earning external respect; relationships are often stable and hierarchical.
- **Dignity Cultures**: Self-worth is self-determined; comfort in direct, rational, fact-based conversation outweighs status.
- **Honor Cultures (Middle East, South Asia, Africa)**: Self-worth is socially claimed; confrontation expresses emotion, and reputation management is paramount.

## Negotiation Skills: High-Performance Collaboration
- **Preparation & Leverage**: Develop and constantly improve your BATNA (Best Alternative to a Negotiated Agreement). Never reveal your BATNA; use it to set your reservation price (bottom line).
- **Positioning**: Set a target goal based on interests and market conditions. If opening second, immediately re-anchor by presenting a strong, prepared counter-proposal.
- **Concessions**: Make numerous small concessions; avoid unilateral moves; maintain a quid pro quo structure.
- **Win-Win Strategy**: Deconstruct the negotiation into multiple issues, prioritize underlying interests (not positions), and devise multi-issue proposals to achieve value-added tradeoffs.

## Conflict De-escalation
- **Interest Strategy**: Focus on understanding the underlying needs and interests ('why' and 'why not') rather than positions or rights.
- **Bringing it Back**: Do not allow discussions to devolve into personal attacks; frame frustration as a matter of importance to the self, not the other party's failings.

## Empathy vs. Sympathy (同理心 vs. 同情心)
- **Empathy (同理心)**: Feeling/imagining oneself into another's humanity; accepting their feeling without judgment, advice, or fixing.
- **Sympathy (同情心)**: Caring about their feeling, but often attempting to comfort or persuade them toward a positive outcome (e.g., saying "at least...").
