> This page explores the relationship between philosophy, theology, and ethics, viewing philosophy as the pursuit of understanding and explaining life's ultimate questions. It integrates concepts from Socratic virtue ethics with broader theological perspectives on living a meaningful life.

## Evolution
- Initial concepts regarding the role of philosophy were being mapped against theological frameworks. This entry refines the view by grounding philosophical inquiry in the lived reality of human existence.

## Content
### Philosophy as Inquiry
Philosophy is defined as love and wisdom, serving as an attempt to explain complex issues and maintaining an open, inclusive posture toward differing viewpoints. It allows philosophy to explore and interpret theology, while theology, in turn, provides a framework for philosophy to remain open and accommodating.

### The Primacy of Life Experience
The pursuit of life itself serves as the primary object of philosophical consideration. The meaning of life is found in the lived experience of being human and striving to live meaningfully. All theoretical discussions gain their ultimate significance from this grounding in lived reality.

### Ethical Framework: Socratic Virtue
Drawing from Socratic thought, virtue ethics emphasizes the pursuit of a moral life.
- **The Mean (中庸)**: Virtue lies in finding the mean between extremes; excess is vice, and deficiency is vice. A virtuous person is one who possesses the judgment to discern what is appropriate in any given situation.
- **Courage (勇敢)**: Courage is not recklessness nor cowardice, but the mean between the two—it is the intellectual virtue that aligns with moral goodness.

### Theological Synthesis
The core concept of seeking a moral life aligns with the Christian philosophical concept of Divine Wisdom. This view suggests that a "rational faith" can guide one toward living a moral life, thereby tempering purely theological debate with practical ethics. This approach shifts the focus from doctrinal purity to the application of belief in shaping virtuous conduct in the temporal world.
