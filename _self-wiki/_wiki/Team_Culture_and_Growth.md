> A successful coaching relationship centers on trusting the individual to perform at their peak while providing necessary tactical instruction when required. A robust team culture accepts failure as a data point, provided lessons are extracted and errors are corrected.

## Evolution
- Initial thoughts focused on the dynamic between a player and a coach, which has been generalized into a principle of high-performing team dynamics.

## Content
### The Role of the Mentor/Coach
The mentor's primary role is to enable the individual to express their highest self and utilize their greatest strengths in the most optimal state. This involves trusting the individual to execute their role while the mentor ensures they are positioned correctly on the "field" (or in the situation).

The mentor provides tactical instruction ("telling you what to do") based on the specific context of the match or challenge.

### Team Culture: Embracing Failure
A powerful team culture, as exemplified by successful models, is built on the acceptance that failure is inevitable. However, this acceptance is conditional: failure must be viewed as a learning opportunity from which lessons are drawn, and errors must be actively corrected.
