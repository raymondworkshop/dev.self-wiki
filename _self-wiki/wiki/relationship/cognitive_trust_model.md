Trust is not a binary switch; it exists on a spectrum requiring careful calibration. Sustainable relationships are built upon a foundation of predictable reliability, where trust is granted incrementally and earned through consistent demonstration of competence and respect.
## Evolution
The initial thoughts on trust were abstract, contrasting ease vs. security (raw/origin-apple-notes/insight.md). This has been formalized into a spectrum: "Controllable Trust" (low-risk testing) leading to "Relational Trust" (high-stakes commitment) (raw/origin-apple-notes/insight.md).
## Key Concepts
*   **Controllable Trust**: Offering small amounts of trust to gauge the partner's stability and allowing oneself to experience being supported without undue risk.
*   **Relational Trust**: This is the high-level agreement that the other party is stable, responsible, and respects boundaries. This trust must be earned over time.
*   **Decision Point**: The decision to trust must be based on a judgment of the system's reliability, not just a feeling.
